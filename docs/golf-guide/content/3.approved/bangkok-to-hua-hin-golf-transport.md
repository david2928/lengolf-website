# Getting from Bangkok to Hua Hin for Golf — All Transport Options

Hua Hin is the top golf destination within easy reach of Bangkok. Sitting roughly 200 km south of the capital, it offers a strong lineup of courses — including [Black Mountain Golf Club](/guide/black-mountain-golf-club-hua-hin) and [Banyan Golf Club](/guide/banyan-golf-club-hua-hin) — alongside a relaxed seaside atmosphere that makes it a natural choice for a golf weekend or short trip. Getting there is straightforward, but if you are travelling with clubs, the transport option you choose matters more than it might for other trips.

This guide covers every realistic way to make the journey, with an honest assessment of what works for golfers and what does not.

---

## Why Transport Choice Matters When You Are Travelling with Clubs

A golf bag is not carry-on luggage. It is large, fragile, and awkward on public transport. Buses and trains are not impossible, but they add friction — loading, unloading, storage space, potential for damage — that most golfers find outweighs the cost saving. If you are bringing your own clubs, a private car or transfer is almost always the right call. If you are renting clubs at the course and travelling light, the public options become genuinely viable.

---

## Option 1: Self-Drive (Rental Car)

**Approx. journey time:** 3 hours (without traffic)
**Best for:** Golfers who want maximum flexibility

Driving yourself gives you total control over timing and stops. The route south from Bangkok follows Highway 35 before joining Highway 4 — well-signed and straightforward once you clear the city. Journey time from central Bangkok to Hua Hin town is approximately 3 hours under normal conditions, though Bangkok traffic can add significantly to that, especially on Friday evenings and public holiday weekends.

All major golf courses in the Hua Hin area have large, free car parks, so arriving by car is seamless. A rental car also means you can move between courses easily during a multi-day trip without arranging transfers each time.

Rental cars are available from Bangkok's airports (Suvarnabhumi and Don Mueang) and from city-centre locations. Verify current rates and availability with the rental provider before your trip, as pricing varies by season.

---

## Option 2: Private Transfer (Hire Car with Driver)

**Approx. journey time:** 3 hours (without traffic)
**Best for:** Golf groups, maximum comfort, door-to-door service

For most golf groups, a private transfer is the most practical and comfortable option. You book a minivan or SUV with a driver, load your bags and clubs at your hotel or the airport, and arrive directly at your resort or first course. There is no parking to arrange, no navigation, and no fatigue from driving before a round.

Private transfers can typically be arranged through your hotel, a local travel agent, or one of the established transfer services operating the Bangkok–Hua Hin route. Prices vary depending on vehicle size and provider — check current rates when booking. For a group of four sharing a vehicle, the per-person cost is often comparable to or only marginally more expensive than the bus, with far greater convenience.

It is worth booking the return transfer in advance, particularly if you are travelling around Thai public holidays, when demand for vehicles on this route is high.

---

## Option 3: Bus

**Approx. journey time:** 3–4 hours
**Best for:** Solo travellers without clubs, budget-conscious trips

Buses to Hua Hin depart from Bangkok's Southern Bus Terminal (Sai Tai Mai), located in the Taling Chan area on the western edge of the city. Multiple operators run this route, with departures throughout the day. Journey time is approximately 3–4 hours, depending on traffic and the number of stops. Tickets are inexpensive and no advance booking is typically required outside of holiday periods.

The bus is a perfectly reasonable choice if you are travelling without clubs and willing to handle your own luggage. It is not well-suited to golf bags — space is limited and handling can be rough. Verify current bus schedules and departure points before travelling, as these can change.

---

## Option 4: Train

**Approx. journey time:** 4–5 hours
**Best for:** Scenic travel; not recommended with clubs

Trains to Hua Hin depart from Hua Lamphong station in central Bangkok and from Bang Sue Grand Station, which has become the main long-distance hub. The journey takes approximately 4–5 hours and passes through the Thai countryside — it is a pleasant trip if you are in no hurry. Fares are low, and the older trains have a certain charm.

In practical terms, travelling by train with a golf bag is awkward. Luggage space is limited, bags must be managed at stations, and the slower journey time offers little compensation. For most golfers, the train is best kept as a leisure option for a return trip without clubs.

## Tips for the Journey

1. **Depart early.** Bangkok traffic heading south can be severe, particularly on Friday afternoons and the eve of public holidays. Leaving before 7 am typically means a cleaner run to the motorway.

2. **Book your return transfer in advance.** Do not assume you can arrange a vehicle on the day of departure, especially during busy periods. Confirm timing and pick-up point with your transfer provider when you book the outbound journey.

3. **Allow buffer time before tee time.** Even in good traffic, arriving tense from a long drive is not ideal. Build in at least 30–45 minutes before your scheduled tee time to park, change, and warm up.

4. **Black Mountain is roughly 25 minutes from Hua Hin town.** If you are heading directly to the course from Bangkok without stopping in town, factor that into your estimated arrival time.
