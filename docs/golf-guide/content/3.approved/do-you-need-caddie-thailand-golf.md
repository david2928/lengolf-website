# Do You Need a Caddie at Thai Golf Courses?

**Yes — at almost all golf courses in and around Bangkok, a caddie is mandatory. You cannot walk the course carrying your own bag. A caddie will be assigned to you before your round begins, regardless of your preference.**

This surprises many visiting golfers who are used to optional caddies or pull-trolley rounds back home. In Thailand, the caddie system is a core part of the golf experience, not an add-on. Here is what you need to know before you play.

---

## 1. What "Mandatory Caddie" Means in Practice

When you check in at a Thai golf course, a caddie will be waiting for you at the first tee or allocated to you at the pro shop. There is no opt-out. The course assigns the caddie — you do not choose one, though regular visitors sometimes request a favourite over time.

A typical loop:

1. Caddie meets you at the bag drop or first tee
2. She carries your bag for the full 18 holes
3. She stays by your side throughout the round
4. After the round, she returns your bag to the bag drop

Most caddies at Bangkok-area courses are women. It is a significant source of employment, and the caddie system is a social institution as much as a logistical one.

---

## 2. The Caddie Fee vs. the Tip — What Is the Difference?

These are two completely separate costs and it is important not to confuse them.

**Caddie fee (mandatory assignment fee)**
- This is the cost of having a caddie assigned to you — it is not a tip
- It is typically included in the green fee package, or charged separately at around 400–600 THB
- You pay this at check-in; it goes to the course, not (or not entirely) to the caddie

**Caddie tip**
- Paid directly to the caddie at the end of the round, in cash
- The standard tip is **400–500 THB per round**
- This is the caddie's primary income and is considered obligatory — not optional
- Always have cash (Thai baht) on you for this purpose

For a full breakdown of tipping norms, see [How Much Should You Tip a Caddie in Thailand?](/faq/how-much-tip-caddie-thailand).

## 3. What Caddies Actually Do

A good Thai caddie is significantly more than a bag-carrier. Here is what you can expect on a typical round:

1. **Carries your bag** for all 18 holes in Bangkok's heat and humidity
2. **Reads greens** — points out slope, grain, and break before you putt
3. **Advises on yardages** — knows the course and will tell you the carry distance to the pin or to hazards
4. **Rakes bunkers** — handles all bunker raking after your shot
5. **Cleans clubs** — wipes down irons and your ball between shots
6. **Manages course logistics** — knows where cart paths are, where to stand, local rules
7. **Offers course management advice** — which side of the fairway to favour, where the trouble is

Even experienced golfers find that a knowledgeable caddie meaningfully improves their score on an unfamiliar layout. Lean into it.

---

## 4. How to Work Well With Your Caddie

A few habits will make the round better for both of you:

1. **Say hello and introduce yourself** — she will be with you for four or five hours
2. **Ask her name early** — use it throughout the round
3. **Tell her your game** — let her know if you hit the ball high or low, how far you carry a 7-iron, whether you have a tendency to miss left or right
4. **Listen to her reads** — especially on greens; she likely plays this course several times a week
5. **Keep your pace** — caddies appreciate golfers who are ready when it is their turn
6. **Tip in cash at the end** — do not pay through the clubhouse or add it to a card

Tipping well builds a reputation over time if you play the same course regularly. A good caddie relationship is one of the genuine pleasures of golf in Thailand. See [Thai Golf Course Etiquette](/guide/thai-golf-course-etiquette) for more on expectations on and off the course.

---

## 5. Exceptions — Courses That Allow Self-Carry

A small number of courses, typically more casual layouts or resort-style facilities outside Bangkok, do permit self-carry or offer pull trolleys. These are the exception rather than the rule.

If self-carry is important to you:
- Call the course directly before booking to confirm their policy
- Do not assume that a lower-priced course means no caddie — many budget courses still assign caddies
- Resort courses catering heavily to international tourists are the most likely to offer self-carry options
