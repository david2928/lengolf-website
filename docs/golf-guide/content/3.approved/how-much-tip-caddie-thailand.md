# How Much to Tip a Caddie in Thailand

**Short answer: tip your caddie 300–400 THB at mid-range courses, or 400–600 THB at premium resorts.** Hand the cash directly to your caddie at the 18th green — not at the pro shop counter.

---

## Quick-Reference Tip Table

| Course Tier | Tip Range (THB) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Public / municipal | 300–400 | Lower end of market |
| Mid-range | 400–500 | Most common amount |
| Premium / resort | 500–600 | Expected baseline at top venues |
| Exceptional service (any tier) | 700–1,000 | Great reads, lost balls found, extra effort in heat |

---

## The Standard Tip Range

Most golfers playing mid-range courses in Thailand leave **400–500 THB** for a standard 18-hole round. This covers normal caddie duties: carrying the bag, cleaning clubs, advising on yardages, and raking bunkers.

At public or municipal tracks where green fees are lower, **300–400 THB** is perfectly respectful. At resort and championship venues — think courses ranked among the best in the region — caddie expectations shift upward and **500–600 THB** is the baseline.

Tipping is not legally required, but it is a firmly established norm in Thai golf culture. Caddies rely on tips as a meaningful part of their income, so withholding a tip without good reason is considered poor etiquette.

## Mandatory Caddie Fee vs. Tip — They Are Not the Same

Almost every golf course in Thailand charges a **mandatory caddie fee**, typically 400–600 THB, collected when you pay your green fee at the counter. This fee goes to the club — not directly to the individual caddie.

The **tip is entirely separate** and is given directly to your caddie in cash at the 18th green. Do not assume the caddie fee covers the tip — it does not, and your caddie will not receive all (or sometimes any) of it.

Budget accordingly: between the mandatory fee and the tip, your total caddie-related cost for a round will typically be **900–1,200 THB** at mid-range courses and **1,000–1,400 THB** at premium venues.

If you are unsure whether a caddie fee has already been included in a package price, ask at the pro shop before you tee off. Either way, bring a separate envelope of cash for the tip.

## When to Tip More

Go above the standard range — 700 THB or higher — when your caddie delivers genuine added value:

1. Exceptional green reading that saves you strokes
2. Helping locate a lost ball in rough or jungle
3. Carrying extra water or snacks on a hot day without being asked
4. Handling a difficult situation (pace of play, other groups, weather) calmly and professionally
5. Going the extra mile on club selection advice throughout the round

Tipping up is always at your discretion. If you had an average round with standard service, the table above is your guide.

---

## Practical Tips: Cash, Timing, and What to Say

1. **Bring THB cash before you arrive.** Most caddies cannot accept card payments and ATMs inside courses are not always available.
2. **Tip at the 18th green** after your final putt, before you return to the clubhouse. This is the universal moment and your caddie will expect it then.
3. **Hand the cash directly** — fold the notes neatly and pass them with both hands or your right hand. A simple *"khob khun krap"* (male speaker) or *"khob khun ka"* (female speaker) — "thank you" in Thai — is always appreciated and goes a long way.
4. **Do not leave the tip on the golf cart or in the bag.** Always hand it over in person.

---

## Group Rounds

In a group round, each golfer tips their own assigned caddie separately. If two golfers share one caddie (unusual but it does happen), a combined tip of 600–800 THB is reasonable depending on the workload and course tier.

Do not pool a single tip and split it — every caddie should receive their own notes directly.
