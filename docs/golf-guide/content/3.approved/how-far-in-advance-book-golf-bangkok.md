# How Far in Advance Should You Book Golf in Bangkok?

Bangkok is one of Southeast Asia's premier golf destinations, with more than 50 courses within an hour of the city centre. Tee time availability varies significantly depending on the day of the week, the time of year, and the course you have in mind. Here is a straightforward breakdown to help you plan.

## The General Rule of Thumb

As a starting point, use these lead times:

- **Weekdays:** 1–3 days in advance is usually sufficient at most public courses.
- **Weekends (Saturday/Sunday):** Book 1–2 weeks ahead to secure your preferred tee time.
- **Peak season (December–February):** Allow 2–4 weeks, especially for popular or well-regarded courses.
- **Premium or private-access courses:** Book as early as possible — 4+ weeks is not unreasonable during busy periods.

The best tee times — generally 6:00 am to 9:00 am — go earliest. If you want an early start, add at least a few extra days to whatever lead time applies to your situation.

## Why Weekends Fill Up Faster

Bangkok has a large and active local golf community. On Saturdays and Sundays, resident golfers, golf societies, and corporate groups compete for the same tee sheets as visiting players. Many courses also offer weekend membership deals that give local players priority booking. As a result, prime weekend slots can be gone 7–10 days out at the most popular venues, and even mid-morning times disappear quickly.

If your schedule is flexible, shifting your round to a Monday or Tuesday often means better availability, faster pace of play, and — at some courses — lower green fees.

## Peak Season: Book Before You Arrive (December–February)

Thailand's cool, dry season from December through February draws the largest number of international visitors, and golf traffic rises sharply with them. During this window, it is strongly advisable to book tee times before you board your flight to Bangkok, not after you land. Courses near popular tourist areas and those frequently listed on booking platforms see the heaviest demand. Waiting until you arrive can leave you with limited choices or inconvenient tee times.

## Same-Day and Walk-In Bookings: Honest Assessment

Walk-in or same-day bookings are sometimes possible, particularly on weekday mornings when a slot opens due to a cancellation. Some smaller or less-promoted courses are more accommodating of spontaneous play. However, relying on this approach is risky. You may end up with a tee time much later than you wanted, or no availability at all at your preferred course. Treat same-day play as a bonus, not a plan.

## Quick Booking Tips

1. **Book weekday rounds** to maximise flexibility and reduce lead times.
2. **Use a reputable booking platform** — see our guide to [how to book golf tee times in Thailand](/guide/how-to-book-golf-tee-times-thailand) for a comparison of options.
3. **Prioritise early tee times** — if you want 7:00 am, book earlier than you think you need to.
4. **Confirm your booking** by phone or email 24–48 hours before your round, especially during peak season.
5. **Research courses first** — our guide to the [best golf courses near Bangkok](/guide/best-golf-courses-near-bangkok) can help you identify which venues require the most advance planning.

Planning ahead is straightforward once you know the patterns. A little lead time goes a long way toward securing the round you want, at the course you want, when you want to play.
