# How Much Does a Round of Golf Cost in Bangkok?

Bangkok is one of the best-value golf destinations in Asia. Whether you are a visiting tourist or an expat looking for a regular game, a full round of golf here costs a fraction of what you would pay in Japan, Australia, or the UK — and the quality of courses is genuinely impressive.

## Quick Answer: All-In Cost Breakdown

A typical weekday round at a mid-range Bangkok course breaks down like this:

| Item | Cost (THB) |
|---|---|
| Green fee (mid-range, weekday) | 2,500 – 3,500 |
| Caddie fee (mandatory) | 300 – 500 |
| Caddie tip (customary) | 200 – 300 |
| Buggy / cart hire (optional) | 300 – 600 |
| **All-in total (est.)** | **3,300 – 4,900** |

At premium courses the all-in figure climbs to 6,000–8,000 THB or more. At budget public courses you can play for closer to 2,000–2,500 THB all-in.

> **Verify before booking:** Green fees change seasonally and are adjusted by individual clubs. Always confirm the current rate directly with the course or through a booking platform before your round.

---

## Green Fees by Tier

Bangkok courses broadly fall into three pricing tiers.

### 1. Budget Courses — 1,500–2,000 THB (weekday)

Older public-access courses and municipally operated clubs sit at the lower end. Facilities are more basic, but the golf is perfectly playable. Expect weekday green fees in the 1,500–2,000 THB range before extras.

### 2. Mid-Range Courses — 2,500–3,500 THB (weekday)

The majority of well-maintained private member courses open to visitors sit in this bracket. These offer good course conditioning, full clubhouses, and organised tee-time systems. This is the sweet spot for most golfers visiting Bangkok.

### 3. Premium Courses — 4,500 THB+ (weekday)

Top-tier clubs such as Nikanti Golf Club and Alpine Golf Club sit at the premium end and are priced accordingly:

- **Nikanti Golf Club:** approximately 5,500 THB weekday / 6,500 THB weekend — all-inclusive (caddie and cart included in the quoted fee)
- **Alpine Golf Club:** approximately 5,400 THB weekday / 7,400 THB weekend — caddie and cart typically included

These courses offer championship-standard layouts, immaculate conditioning, and full resort facilities. Prices listed are indicative — confirm directly with the club before booking.

---

## Mandatory Extras: Caddie Fee and Tip

Unlike in many Western countries, **caddies are compulsory at virtually every Bangkok golf course**. This is non-negotiable and is built into the cost of playing.

- **Caddie fee:** 300–500 THB, charged by the course
- **Caddie tip:** Not mandatory, but strongly expected and culturally important. A standard tip for a satisfactory round is 200–300 THB. For an exceptional caddie — one who reads greens well, carries cheerfully, and keeps you informed — 300–500 THB is appropriate.

Think of the tip not as optional but as the second half of the caddie's pay. Skipping it is noticed and considered poor form.

---

## Optional Extras

### Buggy / Cart Hire

Buggies are optional at most courses (exceptions include courses with compulsory cart policies due to terrain). Expect to pay 300–600 THB for a shared buggy for the round. Walking 18 holes in Bangkok's heat and humidity is possible but demanding — most visitors opt for a cart.

### Food and Drink at the Clubhouse

Clubhouse restaurants range from simple canteen food to full à la carte menus. Budget 200–500 THB for a post-round meal and drinks. Many courses also have drink stations or a marshal's cart on course where water and snacks are available.

---

## Weekday vs Weekend Pricing

Bangkok courses apply a weekend surcharge that is typically 20–40% above the weekday rate. If your schedule allows, playing Monday to Friday delivers meaningfully lower green fees and less crowded tee sheets. Saturday mornings in particular tend to book out early.

---

## Morning vs Twilight Rates

Most courses offer a **twilight rate** for tee times after approximately 2:00–3:00 pm. Twilight green fees are noticeably cheaper than morning rates and suit golfers happy to play a quick 9 or a leisurely 18 finishing around sunset. Check with the specific course for their twilight cut-off time — it varies.

---

## How Bangkok Compares to Golf Elsewhere

Bangkok's green fees are significantly lower than comparable courses in other golf-loving countries:

| Destination | Typical Mid-Range Green Fee |
|---|---|
| Bangkok, Thailand | 2,500–3,500 THB (≈ £55–75 / A$110–140) |
| Tokyo, Japan | ¥15,000–25,000 (≈ £75–125 / A$150–240) |
| Sydney / Melbourne | A$80–180 (≈ £40–90) |
| London, UK | £50–120+ |

Even accounting for mandatory caddie fees, Bangkok consistently offers better value than Japan, and is broadly competitive with mid-market Australian and UK courses — with the added benefit of tropical weather and world-class hospitality.

---

## Tips for Keeping Costs Down

1. **Play weekdays.** The 20–40% weekend premium adds up quickly over a trip.
2. **Book twilight tee times.** Afternoon rates at many courses are 20–30% cheaper than morning slots.
3. **Compare courses on booking platforms.** Aggregators often list promotional rates not available at the pro shop.
4. **Join a group.** Shared buggy costs split two ways halve that line item.
5. **Tip fairly but not excessively.** A 200–300 THB tip is genuinely appreciated and appropriate for a standard round.
