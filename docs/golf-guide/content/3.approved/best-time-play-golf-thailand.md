# Best Time of Year to Play Golf in Thailand

Thailand is one of Asia's top golf destinations, with over 40 courses within an hour of central Bangkok alone. But the country's tropical climate means timing matters. Play at the wrong time of year — or the wrong time of day — and heat, humidity, or afternoon downpours can take the edge off your round. Play it right, and you'll find immaculate fairways, fast greens, and comfortable conditions that make the game genuinely enjoyable.

This guide breaks down Thailand's three seasons, explains what each means for golfers, and gives you a clear recommendation for when to book your tee times.

For a broader overview of playing golf in Thailand, see our [Golf in Thailand Guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide).

## Thailand's Three Golf Seasons

### 1. Cool Season: November to February (Best)

The cool season is, without question, the best time to play golf in Thailand. Temperatures typically sit between 25°C and 32°C, humidity is relatively low, and rainfall is minimal. Mornings are genuinely pleasant — you can walk a full 18 holes without feeling like you've run a marathon.

Bangkok's golf courses are at their busiest during this window, particularly around December and January when visitor numbers peak. That said, the courses are well-staffed and in excellent condition. If you're planning a dedicated golf trip to Thailand, target this season.

**What to expect:**
- Temperatures: 25–32°C
- Humidity: moderate and manageable
- Rain: rare, mostly dry
- Course conditions: best of the year
- Demand: high — book in advance

### 2. Hot Season: March to May (Manageable with Planning)

Once March arrives, temperatures climb fast. By April, Bangkok regularly hits 37–40°C, and standing on an exposed fairway at midday is genuinely draining. This is Thailand's Songkran period (Thai New Year, mid-April), which brings its own festival chaos and a brief uptick in domestic tourism.

Golf is still very playable during the hot season — but only if you're disciplined about tee times. A 6am start means you're finishing before the worst of the heat, and many local golfers follow exactly this approach. Avoid teeing off after 10am.

The upside: fewer international tourists mean quieter courses, and some courses offer better rates during this shoulder period.

**What to expect:**
- Temperatures: 33–40°C
- Humidity: high
- Rain: occasional, increasing toward May
- Course conditions: good, though turf can stress in peak heat
- Demand: moderate — good availability

### 3. Rainy Season: May to October (Challenging but Not Off-Limits)

Thailand's rainy season runs from roughly May to October, driven by the southwest monsoon. In Bangkok, this typically means heavy afternoon thunderstorms rather than all-day rain — mornings are often clear and perfectly playable.

The key adaptation is simple: book early morning tee times. A 7am start in July or August will usually give you 3–4 hours of dry golf before the sky builds up. Many experienced Bangkok golfers play through the entire rainy season this way without much disruption.

That said, conditions are less predictable. Courses can close holes temporarily after heavy overnight rain, and the humidity is relentless even when it's not raining. Fairways can become soft, and you'll want to carry extra dry grips and a rain glove.

For days when the weather simply isn't cooperating — or when you want to escape the heat entirely — Bangkok's indoor golf simulators offer a genuinely useful alternative (more on this below).

**What to expect:**
- Temperatures: 25–33°C
- Humidity: very high
- Rain: afternoon thunderstorms most days; mornings usually dry
- Course conditions: variable — some soft fairways
- Demand: lowest of the year — best availability and rates

## When to Tee Off: Morning and Twilight Times

Regardless of which season you visit, tee time selection has an outsized impact on your experience.

**Morning tee times (6am–9am)** are recommended year-round for international visitors. During the cool season, a 7am start gives you ideal temperatures for your entire round. During the hot and rainy seasons, an early start is essential — it's the difference between an enjoyable round and an endurance test.

Many Bangkok courses open at 6am, and weekend morning slots in peak season fill quickly. Book as early as possible, particularly for courses closer to the city.

**Twilight rates** are widely available at Bangkok-area courses, typically kicking in from 2pm or 3pm onward. These discounted rates — often significantly cheaper than morning green fees — make for an economical option during the cool season when afternoon conditions are still reasonable. Green fees across Bangkok courses vary widely by course and season; expect to find options across a broad range depending on the venue.

## Our Recommendation

**If you have flexibility, visit between November and February.** These four months offer the most comfortable outdoor golf in Thailand, the best course conditions, and pleasant temperatures that let you play at any time of day without the heat becoming an issue. December and January are the sweet spot.

**If you're visiting in the hot or rainy season**, don't be put off — just be strategic. Book the earliest available tee time (6–7am is ideal), carry extra hydration, and use the twilight rate windows during the cool season to stretch your golf budget. Keep an indoor simulator session in reserve as a backup plan.

Bangkok has enough quality golf courses — and enough flexibility in how you play — that any month of the year can yield good golf with the right approach.
