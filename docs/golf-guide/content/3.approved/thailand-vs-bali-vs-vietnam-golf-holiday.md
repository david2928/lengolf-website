# Thailand vs Bali vs Vietnam for a Golf Holiday

Southeast Asia is home to three of the most popular golf destinations outside of Japan and South Korea. Thailand, Bali, and Vietnam each offer compelling reasons to book a flight with your clubs. They also each have genuine drawbacks. This article compares all three honestly, so you can choose the destination that fits your priorities rather than the one with the best marketing.

## Thailand

### Strengths

Thailand has more golf infrastructure than any other destination in the region — roughly 250 to 300 courses nationally, with over 50 within an hour of Bangkok. That density means genuine variety: parkland layouts, resort courses, championship venues, and budget-friendly municipal tracks. Green fees run from around 1,500 THB (~$40 USD) on the low end to 5,000 THB+ (~$135 USD) at marquee venues.

Course quality at the top end is legitimately world-class. Black Mountain Hua Hin, Nikanti Golf Club, Alpine Golf Resort, and Thai Country Club are regularly cited among Asia's best. Bangkok also has a strong indoor simulator scene — [LENGOLF's simulator facility in central Bangkok](https://www.len.golf) lets visitors practice or play full rounds without leaving the city.

The caddie system is a distinctive feature. Caddies are mandatory at most Thai courses, which adds a layer of local knowledge and service that experienced golfers tend to appreciate. The cost is transparent: typically 400–600 THB for the caddie fee plus a 400–500 THB tip as standard.

For seasonal planning, November through February is the sweet spot — cooler temperatures, lower humidity, and reliable dry weather. For more detail, see our guide to the [best time to play golf in Thailand](/guide/best-time-play-golf-thailand).

### Weaknesses

Mandatory caddies mean less flexibility if you prefer to walk alone. Bangkok-area courses can be crowded on weekends, particularly at popular venues. Traffic around Bangkok means early tee times are essential — courses that look close on a map can take 90 minutes to reach during peak hours. Year-round golf is technically possible, but the April–June shoulder period brings heat and occasional afternoon storms that affect pace of play.

## Bali

### Strengths

Bali's appeal as a golf destination is largely about setting. Courses here are built around dramatic volcanic landscapes and rice terrace backdrops that few other destinations can match. For golfers travelling with non-golfing partners, Bali is arguably the strongest option — the non-golf offering (culture, food, wellness, beaches) is exceptionally strong, and courses are typically attached to resort properties that offer everything in one place.

The quality ceiling is genuinely high: Handara Golf Resort has been ranked in Golf Magazine's Top 50 Courses in the World, and New Kuta Golf offers a full championship layout. Green fees at Bali's main 18-hole courses run approximately $80–150 USD. The best season is April through September, the dry season — June through September is the sweet spot.

### Weaknesses

With only around five operational golf courses on the island (as of 2025), choice is the critical limitation. A golfer wanting to play five or six different layouts across a week will exhaust good options immediately. Bali National Golf Club closed in 2025, and Nirwana Bali Golf Club is closed for renovation, further limiting the available roster. Humidity is high even in the dry season. For a serious golfer, Bali works well as a one or two-day add-on or for a mixed trip rather than a dedicated golf destination.

## Vietnam

### Strengths

Vietnam has developed rapidly as a golf destination, and Da Nang in particular has become a genuine draw for serious golfers. The Greg Norman–designed Norman Course at BRG Da Nang Golf Resort (opened 2010) and Montgomerie Links — consistently ranked among Asia's best — anchor the region. Ba Na Hills Golf Club, designed by Luke Donald, has won Asia's Best Golf Course at the World Golf Awards for five consecutive years. Vietnam itself was named Asia's Best Golf Destination for the ninth consecutive year in 2025. With approximately 80–100 courses nationally across Da Nang, Hanoi, and Ho Chi Minh City, there is significantly more variety than Bali.

Green fees at mid-range courses start around $60 USD; Da Nang's top venues charge $120–190 USD per round. The standard of service at top-end venues is high. Vietnam also benefits from a strong food culture and a growing tourism infrastructure that makes it an easy country to navigate.

### Weaknesses

The best golf season varies significantly by region. Da Nang's shoulder seasons can bring heavy rain; Hanoi has cold winters that surprise visitors from warmer climates. Long distances between the three main golf hubs (Hanoi, Da Nang, Ho Chi Minh City) mean you typically need to commit to a region rather than sampling all three. International connectivity to Da Nang specifically, while improving, is not yet on par with Bangkok.

## Verdict by Traveller Type

1. **Budget golfer** — Thailand. The volume of courses keeps fees competitive; good tracks are available from $40 USD.
2. **Serious golfer focused on course quality** — Thailand or Vietnam (Da Nang). Both offer genuine championship-grade venues; Thailand has more of them.
3. **Mixed trip (golf + non-golf partner)** — Bali or Thailand. Bali has a slight edge for resort convenience and setting, but limited course choice (only ~5) means it only works for shorter stays; Thailand wins on course volume for longer trips.
4. **Group trip** — Thailand. Logistics are easier, course choice is broadest, and Bangkok handles large groups well.
5. **First Asia golf trip** — Thailand. Infrastructure, English-language support, and course variety make it the lowest-friction introduction to Asian golf.

## Why Bangkok Works for a City-Based Golf Trip

Most golf destinations in Asia are resort-based, which means leaving the city to play. Bangkok is unusual in having more than 50 courses within roughly an hour of the centre, making it possible to play morning golf and spend evenings in the city without long transfers.

For days when heat or scheduling makes an outdoor round impractical, simulator options like LENGOLF in central Bangkok offer a viable alternative. The broader Thailand golf infrastructure — covered in detail in our [Thailand golf guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide) and our rundown of [the best golf courses near Bangkok](/guide/best-golf-courses-near-bangkok) — makes Bangkok a legitimate home base for a golf-focused trip rather than just a transit stop.
