# Best Time of Day to Play Golf in Bangkok

**Short answer: tee off between 6am and 9am.** This window gives you cooler temperatures, manageable humidity, better pace of play, and enough time to finish before the midday heat peaks. Everything else follows from that.

---

## Why Morning Is the Right Call

Bangkok sits at roughly 13° north of the equator. Heat is not a variable — it is a constant. The gap between a 7am tee time and a 1pm tee time is the difference between a comfortable round and an uncomfortable one.

Key reasons to tee off early:

1. **Temperature** — Bangkok mornings run 25–30°C year-round. By noon that climbs to 32–36°C, and in the hottest months (March–May) afternoon fairways can reach 37–39°C in direct sun.
2. **Humidity** — relative humidity is lower in the morning before solar heating peaks. Sweat dries faster; the air feels less oppressive.
3. **Course conditions** — turf is at its best in the morning, especially on greens. Afternoon play on busy weekends means greens that have been putted on all day.
4. **Finish time** — a 7am start for an 18-hole round (4.5–5.5 hours) has you back at the clubhouse by noon or 12:30, before peak heat and with the rest of the day free.
5. **Traffic** — getting to a course 30–60 minutes from Bangkok requires leaving before rush hour. A 7am tee time means a 5:30–6am hotel departure, which is before Bangkok's worst traffic. Later tee times pull you into the congestion window.

## Weekend vs Weekday Availability

Bangkok-area courses fill up quickly on Saturday and Sunday mornings. The best tee times — 6:30am to 8:30am — are often gone days in advance at popular courses. Book at least a week ahead for a weekend morning slot.

Weekday mornings are more flexible. You can often secure a good tee time with 2–3 days' notice, and the pace of play is noticeably faster with fewer groups on the course.

If your schedule is flexible, a Tuesday or Wednesday morning round gives you the best combination of availability, pace, and value.

---

## Twilight Golf — When It Makes Sense

Some Bangkok-area courses offer discounted afternoon or twilight rates, typically from around 1pm or 2pm onwards. These rates can be 30–50% cheaper than the morning green fee. The trade-off is heat.

Twilight golf makes sense when:

- You are heat-tolerant and hydrate well
- You want to play but couldn't get an early tee time
- You are on a tighter budget and the saving matters
- You only want 9 holes rather than a full 18

It is not well-suited to visitors who have just arrived from a cooler climate, anyone with heat sensitivity, or those planning an evening flight out.

Note that most Bangkok courses have a last tee-off cutoff around 2–3pm to ensure the final group finishes before dark (sunset is around 6–6:30pm year-round in Bangkok).

---

## Seasonal Variation

Bangkok's heat is year-round, but the worst months shift the calculus further toward earlier tee times.

- **November–February (cool season):** 7–9am tee times are comfortable. Afternoon rounds are warm but manageable. This is the peak golf season.
- **March–May (hot season):** Stick strictly to 6–8am tee times. Afternoon play is genuinely punishing. Hydration becomes critical.
- **June–October (wet season):** Morning tee times also dodge the afternoon thunderstorms, which are common from around 2–4pm. Early starts are the safest approach. For more on wet season golf, see the [best time to play golf in Thailand](/guide/best-time-play-golf-thailand) guide.
