# Golf Lessons in Bangkok — Best Coaches and Where to Find Them

Bangkok has a well-developed coaching infrastructure for visiting golfers. The combination of year-round golf, a large expat community, and a serious local golfing culture means that quality instruction is accessible — whether you want one session to fix a specific issue or a structured programme across multiple days.

What makes Bangkok particularly interesting for lessons is the availability of simulator-based coaching, which provides a level of data feedback that most range-based lessons cannot match.

---

## Types of Lessons Available in Bangkok

### 1. Simulator-Based Lessons

The most technically precise coaching option in Bangkok uses a professional-grade golf simulator as the teaching environment. The launch monitor technology captures clubhead speed, ball speed, launch angle, spin rate, carry distance, and face angle data on every shot. A coach working with this data can identify swing faults that are invisible to the naked eye — and more importantly, can verify whether a correction is actually working in real time.

Simulator lessons are particularly effective for:

- Diagnosing the root cause of a recurring shot shape (slice, hook, thin contact)
- Iron compression and angle of attack work
- Driver optimisation (launch angle and spin rate)
- Putting with data feedback on face angle and path

The climate-controlled environment also means lessons are unaffected by Bangkok's heat or the wet season — you can book a 7pm lesson in August with no weather concerns.

### 2. Driving Range Lessons at Bangkok Courses

Most Bangkok-area golf courses offer lessons at their on-site driving range, typically with the club's resident PGA-qualified professional or a senior teaching assistant. These sessions are more traditional — ball striking in an outdoor setting with video feedback and verbal coaching.

Range lessons suit golfers who want to work on feel and rhythm in a full outdoor environment, or who are preparing for a round and want to warm up with structured guidance. Video analysis (using a smartphone or tablet) is standard at most course pro shops today.

### 3. On-Course Playing Lessons

Some coaches offer playing lessons — accompanying you for 9 or 18 holes and coaching in real on-course situations. These are particularly valuable for course management, pre-shot routines, and handling pressure. They are harder to schedule around a visitor itinerary but can be arranged through course pro shops.

---

## What to Look for in a Bangkok Golf Coach

Whether you book at a simulator venue or a course, the same criteria apply:

1. **Qualification** — look for PGA-certified or equivalent qualification. Many Bangkok coaches have trained in the UK, Australia, or the US. Ask about their certification.
2. **Teaching style** — some coaches are highly technical and data-led; others focus on feel and rhythm. Know which suits you before you book.
3. **Video analysis** — any serious lesson today should include video recording from at least two angles. If a coach does not film your swing, that is a red flag.
4. **Communication** — English-language coaching is widely available in Bangkok, particularly at courses that serve international visitors. Confirm before booking.
5. **Specialism** — some coaches focus on beginners; others work primarily with low-handicap players. Match the coach's specialism to your level.

## Driving Range Lessons at Bangkok Courses

The major Bangkok-area courses — Alpine, Nikanti, Thai Country Club, and others — all have driving range facilities and teaching professionals. Booking a lesson here is typically done through the course pro shop, either when you book your round or on a standalone basis.

Range lesson pricing varies by coach and course; confirm current rates directly with the venue.

---

## How Many Lessons Is Realistic on a Typical Trip?

For a 5–7 day golf trip to Bangkok, one or two focused lessons is a realistic target alongside full course days. More than that and you risk overloading your swing with new information mid-trip.

The most effective structure: one simulator session early in the trip to identify a specific issue, then a follow-up session 2–3 days later to reinforce the change. Play rounds in between to embed the adjustment in real conditions.

## Booking Tips

1. Book your lesson slot at the same time as your course tee times — popular coaching slots fill up
2. Specify your handicap and the specific issue you want to address when enquiring
3. If using a simulator lesson, ask in advance whether the coach will provide a post-session data report
4. Allow at least 90 minutes for a first session — 60 minutes of hitting plus debrief time
