# Bangkok BTS Guide for Golfers — Which Line, Which Exit

Bangkok's BTS Skytrain is one of the best urban rail systems in Southeast Asia — fast, clean, air-conditioned, and surprisingly affordable. If you're staying in the city for a golf trip, you'll almost certainly use it. You'll just never use it to get to a golf course.

That's the honest framing for this guide. The BTS is genuinely excellent for city life, but Bangkok's golf courses sit outside the urban rail network entirely. This article explains what the skytrain is actually useful for, where it hands off to Grab, and the one golf-related destination in the city that the BTS does reach directly.

For a broader introduction to getting around Bangkok as a golfer, see our [Bangkok hotels to golf courses transport guide](/guide/bangkok-hotels-to-golf-courses-transport) and the full [golf in Thailand guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide).

## What the BTS Is Actually Useful For

The skytrain runs across two main lines that together cover central Bangkok well.

**1. Sukhumvit Line (light green)**
Runs east to west through the heart of the city, connecting On Nut in the east through Asok, Nana, and Siam, continuing out to Mo Chit and beyond. This is the line most visitors use most often.

**2. Silom Line (dark green)**
Runs from National Stadium through Saladaeng, Sala Daeng, and down to Wongwian Yai. It connects to the Sukhumvit Line at Siam station and intersects with the MRT underground network at Sala Daeng/Si Lom.

Together, these two lines put you within walking distance of:

- **Restaurants and nightlife** — Thonglor, Ekkamai, Asok, and Silom are all BTS-accessible dining and entertainment districts
- **Shopping** — Siam Paragon, CentralWorld, EmQuartier, and Terminal 21 all have BTS stations at the door
- **Sightseeing on rest days** — Chatuchak Weekend Market (Mo Chit), Lumphini Park, and the Chao Phraya river boat piers are all reachable by skytrain or skytrain-plus-short-walk
- **Golf retail** — several sports shops and golf equipment stores sit near BTS stations, useful if you need tees, gloves, or a replacement grip mid-trip

The BTS is also how most golfers move between their hotel and a restaurant the evening before an early tee time — faster and far less stressful than flagging a taxi in peak hour.

## The Reality of Getting to Golf Courses

No Bangkok-area golf course is accessible by BTS. None.

The courses most commonly played on a Bangkok golf trip — layouts to the west, north, east, and south of the city — sit in suburban and semi-rural zones that the skytrain network does not reach. Getting there requires a car. For most visitors, that means Grab.

Grab is the standard answer for golf course transfers in Bangkok. You book through the app, fares are fixed in advance, and drivers are familiar with early morning pickups outside major hotels. A typical run from central Sukhumvit to a course 40–50 km out takes 50–75 minutes depending on traffic and departure time. Early starts (before 6am) make a material difference to journey time. See our full breakdown in the [Grab vs taxi guide for Bangkok golfers](/faq/grab-vs-taxi-bangkok-golf).

## BTS + Grab as a Combination

One approach sometimes suggested is taking the BTS to a major interchange station — Asok, On Nut, or Mo Chit, for example — then ordering a Grab from there to reduce the total fare. In practice, this is almost never worth doing.

The reasons:

1. **Grab from your hotel is already door-to-door.** You have clubs to carry. Adding a BTS leg with a bag on an early morning skytrain before a round is friction for marginal savings.
2. **Fare differences are small.** The savings on a Grab originating from a suburban BTS station versus a central hotel are usually 30–80 baht — not a meaningful sum on a trip that includes green fees.
3. **Timing is harder to control.** With an early tee time, a missed BTS connection or a platform wait adds unpredictable delay.

The combination occasionally makes sense for the return journey — if you're coming back on a quieter afternoon and want to stop somewhere along the BTS before heading to your hotel. But for the outward leg, order Grab from your door.

## LENGOLF — The One Golf Destination on the BTS

There is one golf-related destination in Bangkok that you can reach entirely by skytrain: LENGOLF, Bangkok's indoor golf simulator.

LENGOLF is located in central Bangkok and is accessible directly by BTS, making it the practical option for:

- A session when weather rules out a course round
- An evening warm-up or practice session after sightseeing
- A first-day-in-Bangkok shake-off when you've just landed and aren't ready for an early-morning course trip
- Rest day golf for those who can't stop thinking about their swing

Simulator bays are bookable by the hour, and the facility runs club rental for visitors who haven't brought their full set. It's the only golf option in the city where the BTS gets you all the way there.

## BTS Practical Tips

**Rabbit Card**
Buy a Rabbit Card at any BTS station. It works like an Oyster or Suica card — tap on, tap off, no need to queue for single-trip tickets each time. Top it up at station machines. Cards cost 200 baht total — a 100 baht refundable deposit plus 100 baht of initial credit. The deposit is returned when you surrender the card.

**Fare structure**
Fares are distance-based and range from roughly 17 to 65 baht per journey within the main network (the upper cap increased to 65 baht from November 2025). Transfers between lines are included at Siam station (the main interchange). Transfers to the MRT underground require a separate fare.

**Peak hour crowds**
The BTS is genuinely packed on weekday mornings between roughly 7:30am and 9am, and again from 5pm to 7pm. If you're heading to a course on a weekday and taking Grab, leave before the window — both to beat BTS crowds (if you're walking to a main road) and to beat the expressway traffic.

**MRT connection**
The MRT underground network (blue and yellow lines) connects with the BTS at several interchange stations. If your hotel is closer to an MRT station, you can ride the MRT to a BTS interchange and continue from there. The systems use separate cards and fares.

---

The BTS is worth understanding as a golfer in Bangkok even if you never use it for course transfers. It gives you independent movement around the city — restaurants, shopping, rest-day sightseeing — without depending on taxis or ride-hailing for every journey. For the courses themselves, that's Grab's job. And for simulator golf in the city, LENGOLF has a BTS-accessible bay waiting.
