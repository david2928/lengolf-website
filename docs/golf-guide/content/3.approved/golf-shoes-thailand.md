# Golf Shoes in Thailand — Do You Need to Bring Your Own?

**Short answer: yes, bring your own golf shoes.** Rental footwear exists at some courses, but availability is inconsistent enough that you should not rely on it. Packing a pair you trust is the safer and more comfortable choice.

## What Most Thai Golf Courses Require

The vast majority of Bangkok-area courses enforce a proper footwear policy on their fairways. That means dedicated golf shoes — either spiked or spikeless — rather than running shoes, sneakers, flip flops, or sandals. Policies do vary: a handful of more relaxed municipal or resort courses will permit clean athletic trainers, but this is the exception rather than the rule. If you plan to play multiple venues without checking each one in advance, assume golf shoes are required.

**Bottom line:** Do not show up in flip flops or sandals. You are almost certain to be turned away.

## Spikeless vs. Spiked in Thailand's Conditions

Thailand's heat and humidity tip the balance firmly toward **spikeless shoes** for most visiting golfers.

- **Spikeless shoes** are lighter, breathe better, and dry faster — advantages that matter a great deal when temperatures regularly sit above 30 °C. They also double as casual footwear off the course, saving space in your bag.
- **Spiked shoes** provide maximum traction, which can be an advantage on courses that stay wet during the rainy season (roughly May–October). If you play predominantly during that window and prioritise grip on sodden fairways, spiked shoes remain a solid choice.

For most visitors playing a mix of dry and wet season rounds, a quality waterproof or water-resistant spikeless shoe covers both scenarios well.

## Rental Shoe Availability — Do Not Count on It

Some courses include rental golf shoes as part of a full caddie/equipment package; others offer them at the pro shop for a small fee. However, stock is limited, sizing tends to skew toward smaller Asian fit widths, and hygiene quality varies. It is not unusual to arrive and find no shoes in your size, or shoes that have seen better days.

If rental shoes are genuinely your only option, call the course ahead of your round to confirm availability and sizing. Do not leave it to chance on the day.

Playing on an **indoor golf simulator** at [LENGOLF](/golf-in-thailand-guide) removes the footwear question entirely. The simulator bays are played on artificial turf mats rather than natural grass, so there is no shoe requirement beyond being clean and comfortable. Regular trainers or casual shoes are perfectly fine — no need to carry golf shoes to your session.

## Packing Tip: Spikeless Shoes Are a Smart Space Trade-Off

Golf shoes are the bulkiest single item most golfers pack. Choosing a versatile spikeless pair lets you wear them to dinner, around the resort, or exploring after your round — effectively replacing a second pair of casual shoes. That trade-off makes the bag space feel far less costly. Look for a low-profile spikeless model in a neutral colour and it will serve you equally well on and off the fairway.
