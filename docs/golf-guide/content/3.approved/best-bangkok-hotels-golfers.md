# Best Bangkok Hotels for Golfers — Stay Central, Play Anywhere

Bangkok is one of the best bases in Southeast Asia for a golf trip — not because the city itself has courses, but because it sits at the centre of a region with over 50 courses within an hour's drive. Choose the right neighbourhood to stay in, know what to ask your hotel, and you can tee off at world-class layouts in every direction without changing accommodation once.

This guide covers where to stay, what to look for, and how to make the most of Bangkok's infrastructure as a golfer. For a full overview of the regional course options, see our [golf in Thailand guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide).

---

## Best Areas to Stay for Golfers

Bangkok's layout matters for golfers. Courses are spread across four compass points — west toward Nakhon Pathom (Nikanti Golf Club, roughly one hour), north toward Pathum Thani (Alpine Golf Club, around 30 minutes), and east toward Chonburi (Thai Country Club, around one hour). Staying in a central district keeps all of those options roughly equidistant.

**1. Sukhumvit**
The most popular area for visitors, Sukhumvit sits in the eastern half of the city centre and offers excellent transport links. It has the highest concentration of international-standard hotels, restaurants, and golf outfitters. Access to courses in the north and east is straightforward. Western routes can involve heavier traffic, so factor in an earlier departure on those days.

**2. Silom and Sathorn**
The business district trades some of Sukhumvit's convenience for a quieter, more efficient feel. Hotels here tend to have attentive concierge teams familiar with corporate guests who want early-morning transfers. The expressway network from Sathorn gives reasonable access to all directions, though as with anywhere in Bangkok, early departure (before 6am) is the real equaliser.

**3. Riverside**
Staying along the Chao Phraya River puts you in one of Bangkok's most scenic and relaxed zones. Riverside hotels often have more land and better facilities for club storage. The trade-off is that expressway access can be slightly less direct for northern courses, though for western and southern routes the riverside is well-positioned.

For any of these areas, the single most important factor is not the exact address — it's how the hotel handles logistics for early-morning departures.

## What to Look For in a Golf-Friendly Hotel

Not every hotel that markets itself to golfers actually makes the day easier. When evaluating options, ask about these specifics before booking:

1. **Club storage** — Can the hotel store bags securely between rounds, including oversized travel bags? Is there a dedicated sports storage room or just a general luggage hold?
2. **Early breakfast** — Tee times at 6–9am are ideal in Bangkok's climate, which means leaving the hotel by 5–6am. Confirm whether the kitchen opens early enough, or whether grab-and-go options are available.
3. **Transfer arrangements** — Does the hotel have preferred drivers or a transport desk that can book private transfers to golf courses? What is the lead time required?
4. **Concierge knowledge** — A concierge who has actually arranged golf transfers before will know which routes to take at different times of day. Ask specifically whether they have experience booking tee times at courses outside the city.
5. **Late check-in and bag storage on departure day** — Golf trips often involve an afternoon flight after a morning round. Can the hotel hold bags after checkout so you can come back, shower, and leave?

These are operational questions, not luxury questions. A mid-range hotel that handles all five well will serve a golf trip better than a five-star property with no sports infrastructure.

## The 5-Star Concierge Advantage

At Bangkok's top-end properties, the concierge desk is a genuine asset for golfers. Senior concierges at established five-star hotels typically maintain direct relationships with the starter desks at the major courses. They can book tee times that would otherwise require navigating foreign-language booking pages, and they often know the best slots before availability opens to the public.

They can also brief drivers on route options — an important detail when courses like Thai Country Club involve navigating expressway toll plazas and course-specific approach roads that aren't always well-signed. A well-briefed driver will know where to drop you, where the bag drop is, and how to time the return pickup.

If you are staying at a five-star property, introduce yourself to the concierge the evening you arrive and run through your full schedule. Give them the course names, the dates, your preferred tee time windows, and any flexibility you have. Let them make the calls.

## Budget vs Luxury — What Each Gives Up

Bangkok offers genuine quality at both ends of the hotel market. A well-chosen mid-range property in Sukhumvit or Silom can handle golf logistics competently. What budget hotels typically sacrifice:

- **1.** Concierge depth — staff may be helpful but lack course-specific knowledge
- **2.** Club storage security — bags may go into a general luggage room rather than a dedicated sports hold
- **3.** Early kitchen access — grab-and-go options are less reliable
- **4.** Negotiated transfer rates — you may need to arrange private transport independently

Luxury hotels sacrifice very little for golfers, but the premium is real. The practical middle ground is a well-reviewed four-star property in a central business district with a dedicated concierge team and confirmed early breakfast availability.

## Practical Tips for a Smoother Golf Trip

1. **Store clubs at the hotel throughout the trip** — Bangkok's traffic makes lugging clubs to and from a storage facility impractical. Confirm storage is available before you arrive.
2. **Request late bag storage on departure day** — most hotels accommodate this, but confirm in advance to avoid confusion at checkout.
3. **Negotiate transfer rates upfront** — agree a flat rate for the full day (drop off, wait, return) rather than paying per journey. Drivers who wait at courses are standard practice.
4. **Leave earlier than you think you need to** — Bangkok's expressways can move well at 5am and be congested by 7am. For courses an hour away, a 5:30am departure is not excessive.
5. **Confirm tee time and dress code the day before** — courses in Thailand are generally relaxed, but some require collared shirts and soft spikes. Your hotel concierge can verify this.
