# Thailand Visa Guide for Golf Tourists

> **Disclaimer:** Visa rules change frequently. All information below reflects the situation as of early 2026 and is provided as general guidance only. Always verify your specific requirements with your nearest Thai embassy, consulate, or the official Thai immigration website before booking travel.

---

## Quick Answer: Do You Need a Visa for a Golf Trip?

For most visitors, a golf holiday in Thailand requires nothing more than a standard tourist entry — there is no special golf visa or sports permit. The key question is simply which entry pathway applies to your passport.

Thailand offers three main routes in:

1. **Visa Exemption** — arrive without any prior visa and receive a stamp at the border
2. **Visa on Arrival (VOA)** — obtain a short-stay visa at the airport on arrival
3. **e-Visa** — apply online before you travel through the official Thai e-Visa portal

One of these three will cover your trip. Which one depends entirely on your nationality — read on for how to find out which applies to you.

---

## The Three Entry Pathways

### 1. Visa Exemption

Many nationalities can enter Thailand without applying for any visa in advance. Visitors entering under visa exemption receive a stamp on arrival that permits a stay for tourism purposes. Golf holidays fall comfortably within the activities permitted under tourist entry.

**Do not rely on any list you find online (including this one) for exemption eligibility.** Exemption arrangements are subject to change. Check the official Thai Ministry of Foreign Affairs website or contact your nearest Thai embassy.

### 2. Visa on Arrival (VOA)

Nationalities not covered by visa exemption may be eligible for a Visa on Arrival at designated international airports, including Suvarnabhumi (BKK) and Don Mueang (DMK). The VOA is issued at the airport on the day you arrive.

To apply for VOA you will typically need:
- A completed arrival/VOA application form
- A passport-sized photograph
- Evidence of onward travel (return or connecting flight)
- Proof of sufficient funds for your stay
- Payment of the VOA fee (payable in Thai baht at the counter — verify the current fee before travel as amounts change)

Queues at the VOA counter can be long, particularly at Suvarnabhumi during peak season. Factor this into your arrival planning if you have an early tee time.

### 3. e-Visa

Thailand's e-Visa system allows eligible nationalities to apply for a tourist visa online before departing. This is worth considering if you want to avoid airport queues or if VOA is not available for your passport. Apply through the official Thai e-Visa portal well in advance of travel.

## What to Check Before You Travel

Before booking flights, confirm the following:

1. **Your entry pathway** — check the Thai Ministry of Foreign Affairs website or your country's Thai embassy for the current rules for your specific passport.
2. **Passport validity** — most airlines and Thai immigration officers require at least six months of remaining validity on your passport from your date of entry. Check your passport expiry well in advance.
3. **Onward or return ticket** — immigration officers and airlines routinely ask for evidence that you will leave Thailand before your permitted stay expires. Carry your return flight confirmation.
4. **Sufficient funds** — Thailand's immigration rules include a requirement to demonstrate you have adequate funds for your stay. The specific amounts are published on official immigration channels.
5. **Multiple entries** — if you plan to cross into a neighbouring country and return to Thailand during one trip, check whether your entry type permits re-entry.

---

## At the Airport: What to Expect on Arrival

Most golf visitors to Bangkok will arrive at one of two airports:

- **Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK)** — Bangkok's main international hub, approximately 30 km east of the city centre. The majority of long-haul international flights land here.
- **Don Mueang Airport (DMK)** — Bangkok's secondary airport, north of the city, used primarily by low-cost carriers operating regional Asian routes.

Both airports have dedicated immigration halls. If you are eligible for visa exemption, proceed directly to the immigration counters. If you require a Visa on Arrival, follow signs to the VOA counter before joining the main immigration queue.

**Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC):** As of May 2025, the old paper TM6 form has been replaced by the Thailand Digital Arrival Card. Most foreign visitors must complete this online within 72 hours before their flight — it is free and submitted at [tdac.immigration.go.th](https://tdac.immigration.go.th/). You will receive a QR code to present at the immigration counter on arrival.

At the immigration counter, you will present your passport, your TDAC QR code, and any supporting documents (onward ticket, accommodation details). Processing is typically straightforward for tourist visits.

## How Long Can You Stay?

The permitted length of stay varies by nationality and entry type. It is not possible to state a single duration that applies to all visitors. As of early 2026:

- Stays under visa exemption vary by passport nationality
- VOA and e-Visa durations are set by the specific visa category issued

Check the official Thai immigration website for the duration applicable to your situation. If you wish to extend your stay, extensions can often be applied for at a Thai immigration office in-country, but extension conditions and fees should be verified with the immigration authority directly.

## Practical Tips for Golf Tourists

Golf trips to Thailand are straightforward from a visa perspective — there is nothing specific to golf that creates additional entry requirements. A few practical notes:

1. **No golf-specific visa exists** — a standard tourist entry covers all golf activities, whether at public courses, private clubs, or indoor simulators like LENGOLF in Bangkok.
2. **Equipment import** — travelling with your own clubs is common and generally uncomplicated for tourists. There is no duty applied to personal-use golf equipment brought in for your trip. Confirm current customs rules with the Thai Customs Department if you are bringing a large quantity of equipment.
3. **Book tee times in advance** — Bangkok's popular courses fill up, particularly on weekends. Visa and entry logistics aside, early booking is the main practical consideration for golf tourists.
4. **Indoor golf in Bangkok** — if you arrive late or want a session without travelling to a course, LENGOLF operates indoor golf simulator bays in central Bangkok. No additional permissions beyond your tourist entry are required — it is simply a leisure venue.
