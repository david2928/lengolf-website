# Golf Club Rental in Bangkok — Where to Rent and What It Costs

One of the most practical advantages of golfing in Bangkok is how easy it is to pick up a rental set. Whether you're flying in light, don't want to deal with airline baggage fees, or simply want to try a different set, renting clubs in Bangkok is straightforward and widely available. Most golfers visiting Thailand are pleasantly surprised by the quality on offer — especially at better-rated courses and dedicated simulator venues.

This guide covers where to rent, what quality to expect, what it typically costs, and what to check before you tee off.

---

## Where to Rent Golf Clubs in Bangkok

### 1. At the Golf Course Pro Shop

The most common option for visiting golfers is renting directly from the course. Virtually every 18-hole course in and around Bangkok has rental sets available at the pro shop, and most will have at least two or three sets to choose from. You book your round, arrive at the course, and pick up a set alongside your caddie assignment — it's a seamless part of the standard course experience.

Quality varies significantly by venue. Budget courses may offer older, mixed sets with mismatched shafts. Mid-range and premium courses typically stock newer equipment from major brands such as Callaway, TaylorMade, or Titleist. If you're playing somewhere well-regarded, you can generally expect playable, well-maintained equipment.

For more detail on the rental experience at Thai courses specifically, see our guide to [renting golf clubs at Thai golf courses](/guide/renting-golf-clubs-thai-golf-courses).

### 2. Dedicated Golf Simulator Venues

Indoor golf simulator venues in Bangkok — such as LENGOLF — offer rental clubs as part of their session packages. This option is particularly useful if you want to practice, play a virtual round, or warm up before heading out on a real course. Simulator venues tend to invest in quality rental equipment because club performance directly affects the playing experience on screen.

This is also a practical choice if you want to try a specific brand before committing to a purchase, or if you're new to golf and aren't ready to travel with your own set.

### 3. Golf Equipment Shops

For longer trips or golfers who prefer consistency across multiple rounds, some golf retail shops in Bangkok offer short-term rentals or demo programs. This is less common than course or simulator rental, but worth exploring if you'll be playing frequently over one to two weeks and want the same clubs each time. Major golf retail areas in Bangkok include Siam Paragon and MBK Center, which house several dedicated golf shops.

---

## What to Expect Quality-Wise

The quality of rental clubs in Bangkok follows a fairly predictable pattern:

- **Budget courses:** Older iron sets, limited options, mixed shaft flex. Functional but not ideal for scoring.
- **Mid-range courses:** Decent condition, usually a full set of irons plus woods and a putter. Name-brand clubs more likely.
- **Premium courses and simulator venues:** Newer equipment, multiple set options, often separated by gender and handedness. Brands like Callaway, TaylorMade, and Titleist are common.

If you're particular about equipment, it's always worth calling ahead to ask what's currently available.

---

## Cost Overview

Rental set prices at Bangkok golf courses generally fall in the range of 300–800 THB per round, though this varies depending on the course tier and what's included. Some courses bundle the rental fee into caddie and green fee packages; others charge separately.

Dedicated simulator venues price club rental differently — typically it's included in the per-bay or per-hour session rate rather than charged as a standalone item.

For context on overall golf costs in Bangkok, see [how much a round of golf costs in Bangkok](/guide/round-of-golf-cost-bangkok).

Always verify rental pricing directly with the venue before your visit, as rates change and what's listed online may not reflect current fees.

## What to Check When Renting

Before you accept a rental set, take a few minutes to review the following:

1. **Shaft flex** — Regular flex suits most recreational players. If you have a faster swing, ask if stiff flex is available.
2. **Grip condition** — Worn or slick grips significantly affect control. Check that grips are tacky and not cracked.
3. **Full set vs partial** — Confirm you're receiving a complete set: driver, fairway woods or hybrids, a full iron set, wedges, and a putter. Some budget rentals omit certain clubs.
4. **Left-handed availability** — Left-handed sets are less common at courses. Call ahead if you need them — don't assume they'll have stock on the day.
5. **Ladies' sets** — Most courses carry ladies' sets, but again, availability and quality vary. Premium venues are more reliable here.

1. **Men's (right-handed)**
2. **Ladies'**
3. **Left-handed**

This makes LENGOLF a particularly good option for left-handed golfers, who often find rental options limited at standard courses. The Callaway sets are well-maintained and suitable for all ability levels — from complete beginners hitting their first shots to experienced players working on specific aspects of their game.

Club rental is included with simulator bay bookings, so there's no separate fee to track.

## Tips for Getting the Best Rental Experience

1. **Call or message ahead** — Confirm what rental sets are available, especially if you need left-handed clubs or ladies' clubs.
2. **Arrive a few minutes early** — Give yourself time to check the clubs before your tee time rather than rushing straight to the first hole.
3. **Ask the caddie** — At courses with caddies, your caddie can often swap out individual clubs if something isn't right. They know what's in the bag room.
4. **Consider bringing your own wedges** — Rental wedges tend to show the most wear. If you travel with just one or two clubs, a trusted wedge is the most impactful choice.
5. **Don't over-rent** — If you're a beginner, a half-set (irons 5–PW plus a driver and putter) is easier to manage than a full 14-club set.
