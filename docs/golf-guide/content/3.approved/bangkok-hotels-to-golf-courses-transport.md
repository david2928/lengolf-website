# Getting from Bangkok Hotels to Golf Courses

Bangkok has more than 50 golf courses within an hour of the city centre — which sounds perfect until you check the time it takes to actually get there. Traffic is the defining variable of any golf morning in Bangkok. Get it right and you arrive relaxed with time to warm up. Get it wrong and you're watching your tee time tick past from the back of a stationary taxi. This guide gives you the practical framework to plan departures, choose the right transport, and reach any course comfortably.

For a broader overview of courses in the region, see the [golf in Thailand guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide).

---

## Courses by Direction and Approximate Journey Time

Bangkok's courses are spread across four compass points. Understanding the direction and distance from your hotel is the starting point for any transport plan.

**1. North — Pathum Thani (Alpine Golf Club)**
Alpine Golf Club sits roughly 30 minutes north of central Bangkok under ideal conditions. It is the closest major course to the city, and the expressway connection from most Sukhumvit or Silom hotels is direct. For a 7am tee time, departing by 6:00am gives comfortable margin during peak traffic hours.

**2. West — Nakhon Pathom (Nikanti Golf Club)**
Nikanti is approximately one hour west of Bangkok, and the western routes through the city carry some of the heaviest morning congestion. Factor in extra time on weekday mornings. For a 7am tee time, depart no later than 5:30am. Weekend departures can be slightly more forgiving, but erring early is always the safer approach.

**3. East — Chonburi (Thai Country Club)**
Thai Country Club is around one hour east, accessed via the elevated expressway and the motorway toward Chonburi. The route is generally reliable but will slow significantly if you are caught in outbound commuter traffic heading toward the eastern suburbs and industrial zones. For a 7am tee time, plan to leave by 5:45am–6:00am.

**4. South and South-East**
A handful of courses sit south and south-east of the city. Journey times vary between 45 minutes and 90 minutes depending on the specific course and your hotel location. Check routing via Google Maps or Grab the evening before — departure times shift considerably based on exact destination.

For ranked recommendations on which courses to target, see [best golf courses near Bangkok](/guide/best-golf-courses-near-bangkok).

## Transport Options

**Option 1: Grab (most flexible)**
Grab is the dominant ride-hailing app in Bangkok and works reliably across the city. It handles early-morning pickups well, allows you to pre-book, and shows estimated fares and journey times before you confirm. The app lets you track your driver and share your ETA — useful when you have a tee time booked and cannot be late.

Practical notes for using Grab to reach golf courses:
- Pre-book the night before if your tee time is before 7am
- Add the course address in both English and Thai to avoid confusion
- Confirm the pickup point clearly if your hotel has multiple entrances
- Carry a screenshot of the course address as a backup

**Option 2: Hotel Transfer (most convenient for 5-star guests)**
Most 5-star hotels in Bangkok can arrange a dedicated golf transfer through their concierge or guest services team. This is the most seamless option — the driver knows where he is going, the vehicle is comfortable, and you often have club storage handled from door to door. Costs are higher than Grab but the convenience is genuine.

Ask your hotel concierge at check-in whether they offer golf transfers, what the lead time is for booking, and whether they have a regular arrangement with any specific courses. Hotels with a strong corporate client base tend to have this well organised.

**Option 3: Rental Car (best for multi-course itineraries)**
If you are playing multiple courses across several days and want maximum flexibility — including the ability to detour, store equipment overnight in the vehicle, and leave at exactly the time you choose — a rental car is worth considering. Self-driving in Bangkok city traffic takes some adjustment, but the expressway network to the outlying course areas is straightforward once you are out of the inner city.

This option suits golfers on week-long trips who are comfortable driving in Thailand. For single-day trips or short stays, Grab or a hotel transfer is simpler.

## Traffic Timing Guide

Bangkok's rush hour is most severe between 7:00am and 9:00am outbound from the city, and 5:00pm to 8:00pm inbound. For golf purposes, the morning window is the critical one.

| Direction | Course Example | Approx. Journey | Depart by (7am tee time) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| North | Alpine Golf Club | 30 min | 6:00am |
| West | Nikanti Golf Club | 60 min | 5:30am |
| East | Thai Country Club | 60 min | 5:45am |
| South / SE | Various | 45–90 min | 5:30am–6:00am |

These times assume a central Bangkok hotel (Sukhumvit, Silom, or Sathorn). If your hotel is on the city periphery, adjust accordingly.

For later tee times (9am or beyond), you can afford a slightly later departure, but the window between the end of rush hour and your tee time narrows quickly. When in doubt, leave earlier. Arriving at a course 30 minutes ahead of schedule is a benefit — it gives you time to warm up, confirm your caddie assignment, and sort rental clubs if needed. For guidance on booking and what to expect on arrival, see [how to book golf tee times in Thailand](/guide/how-to-book-golf-tee-times-thailand).

## The No-Travel Option: LENGOLF in Central Bangkok

If you want to play golf without any of the logistics above, LENGOLF is the straightforward answer. Located in central Bangkok, it requires no early departure, no traffic calculation, and no transport arrangement. It is a premium golf simulation facility — you book a bay, show up, and play. The experience is purpose-built for golfers who want to hit balls, work on their game, or play a simulated round without leaving the city.

It is not a replacement for getting out to a full course, but for a session when you do not have three hours to spare on logistics, or when Bangkok traffic has no appeal, it is a practical and enjoyable alternative.

---

Bangkok golf logistics are manageable once you accept the core principle: traffic is real, departure times matter, and planning the evening before takes less time than dealing with the consequences of leaving late. Use Grab for flexibility, your hotel concierge for comfort, and a rental car if you are building a multi-course trip. Everything else follows from leaving early enough.
