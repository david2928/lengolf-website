# Thailand Entry Requirements 2026 — Quick Guide for Golfers

> **Disclaimer:** Entry requirements can change without notice. The information below reflects the situation as understood in early 2026. Always verify current rules with the [Thai Immigration Bureau](https://www.immigration.go.th/) or your nearest Thai embassy before travel.

---

## Pre-Flight Checklist for Golfers

Before you board, confirm you have the following:

1. **Valid passport** — Thai immigration recommends at least 6 months validity beyond your intended departure date
2. **Onward or return ticket** — evidence of onward travel is standard practice at immigration
3. **Accommodation details** — hotel name and address for your first night
4. **Proof of funds** — sufficient funds for your stay (amount is at the discretion of the officer; carry a bank card and some cash)
5. **Travel / health insurance** — not mandatory, but strongly recommended (see below)
6. **Golf equipment** — no special permit required; standard customs rules apply (see below)

No COVID-related requirements remain in place as of 2026. There are no vaccination certificates, health declarations, or testing requirements for entry into Thailand.

---

## What's Changed Since COVID

Thailand removed all pandemic-era entry restrictions in 2022. As of 2026, arriving golfers face the same straightforward process that existed before the pandemic:

- No COVID vaccination proof required
- No pre-arrival health declarations
- No testing on arrival

The paper **TM6 arrival card** has been replaced by the **Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC)**, which became mandatory for most foreign arrivals as of May 2025. The TDAC must be completed online within 72 hours before your flight — it is free and submitted via the official Thai Immigration portal at [tdac.immigration.go.th](https://tdac.immigration.go.th/). You will receive a QR code to present at immigration on arrival.

**Visa exemption and visa-on-arrival options** exist for many nationalities, but the specific countries covered, permitted stay durations, and fees are subject to change. Verify your nationality's current entitlement at [official Thai immigration sources](https://www.immigration.go.th/) before booking — do not rely solely on this article or third-party travel blogs for this information.

## Golf Equipment at Customs

Travelling with a full set of clubs, a travel bag, and accessories is routine at both of Thailand's main Bangkok airports — **Suvarnabhumi (BKK)** and **Don Mueang (DMK)** — and no special golf equipment declaration or permit is required.

Standard Thai customs rules apply:

- **Personal-use equipment** (clubs you own and are bringing for your own play) passes through without issue in normal circumstances
- **High-value or new equipment** — if you are bringing brand-new clubs still in retail packaging, or equipment with a combined value that may attract customs duty, you may be asked to declare the items. When in doubt, declare; the penalties for undisclosed dutiable goods are more inconvenient than the declaration itself
- **Renting at the course** is a straightforward alternative if travelling light — rental sets are available at most Bangkok-area courses, including at [LENGOLF](/golf-in-thailand-guide)

There is no requirement to obtain a separate import permit or golf-specific customs waiver.

## Health and Travel Insurance

Health insurance is not a mandatory entry requirement for Thailand. That said, it is strongly recommended for any golf trip:

- Medical treatment at private hospitals in Bangkok is excellent but not inexpensive for uninsured patients
- A golf-related injury (strained back, heat exhaustion, or a slip on a wet course) can result in costs that far exceed the premium of a travel policy
- Some travel insurance policies exclude sports activities — check that golf is covered before purchasing

Look for a policy that includes medical evacuation cover if you are travelling from a distant country.

## Official Sources to Verify Before You Travel

Requirements change. Before finalising your travel plans, check:

1. **Thai Immigration Bureau** — [www.immigration.go.th](https://www.immigration.go.th/)
2. **Your country's Thai embassy or consulate** — for visa requirements specific to your nationality
3. **Thai Customs Department** — [www.customs.go.th](https://www.customs.go.th/) for the latest on goods declaration thresholds
