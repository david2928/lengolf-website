# Golf Weather in Bangkok by Month

Bangkok sits just 14 degrees north of the equator, which means it is warm all year. For golfers, that is mostly good news — there is no off-season, snow, or frost. What you do need to plan around is the tropical rainfall cycle, which divides the calendar into three distinct phases: a cool dry season, a hot dry season, and a long rainy season defined by afternoon thunderstorms. Get the timing right and you can play 40+ courses within an hour of central Bangkok in near-perfect conditions. Get it wrong and you may spend your round sheltering under a rain tree.

This guide breaks down golf weather in Bangkok by every month of the year so you can match your travel dates to the conditions that suit you best. For a broader look at timing a Thailand golf trip, see our [complete golf in Thailand guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide) and our [best time to play golf in Thailand](/guide/best-time-play-golf-thailand) article.

## Month-by-Month Golf Weather Guide

### January — Cool Season

**Temperature:** 24–32°C | **Humidity:** Low | **Rain:** Very low

January is Bangkok's peak golf month. Morning temperatures are genuinely cool by Thai standards — low-to-mid 20s with a breeze — and afternoon highs rarely push above 32°C. Humidity is minimal, skies are blue, and rain events are rare. Courses are in excellent condition after a full rainy season of growth.

**Tee time advice:** Any time of day is comfortable. A 7–8am start lets you finish before the midday sun intensifies.

**Golf rating: 5/5**

---

### February — Cool Season

**Temperature:** 25–33°C | **Humidity:** Low | **Rain:** Very low

February closely mirrors January. Temperatures begin to inch upward through the month, but conditions remain very good. Mornings are still pleasantly cool and afternoon play stays manageable. This is the last full month before the heat begins to build.

**Tee time advice:** Early morning tee times (6–9am) are ideal. Twilight rates from ~2–3pm offer good value if you prefer afternoon light.

**Golf rating: 5/5**

---

### March — Hot Season

**Temperature:** 27–34°C | **Humidity:** Moderate | **Rain:** Low

March marks the start of the hot season. Average highs climb to around 34°C and the air feels noticeably thicker than February. Morning golf is still excellent — it is worth setting the alarm early. Midday and afternoon rounds can be taxing, so build in extra water and use shade wherever the course offers it.

**Tee time advice:** 6–8am start is strongly recommended. Avoid tee times after 11am.

**Golf rating: 4/5**

---

### April — Hot Season (Peak Heat)

**Temperature:** 28–35°C | **Humidity:** Moderate–High | **Rain:** Low to moderate

April is the hottest month of the year. Average highs sit around 35°C and can push past 40°C during the occasional extreme heat spikes. Songkran (Thai New Year) falls in April, which brings festive crowds but also means many Thais are travelling — booking ahead is wise. Golf is absolutely playable, but only if you start early.

**Tee time advice:** A 6–7am tee time is almost essential. Carry at least 2 litres of water per player.

**Golf rating: 3/5**

---

### May — Transition: Hot to Rainy

**Temperature:** 27–34°C | **Humidity:** High | **Rain:** Moderate, increasing

May straddles the hot and rainy seasons. Humidity rises sharply, and the first afternoon thunderstorms of the year begin arriving. Rain is not yet daily or reliable, but when storms do come they are intense. Mornings are still largely dry and often beautiful.

**Tee time advice:** 6–9am tee times give you the highest chance of completing a dry round. Keep an eye on the skies by late morning.

**Golf rating: 3/5**

---

### June — Rainy Season

**Temperature:** 26–33°C | **Humidity:** 75–80% | **Rain:** High

The rainy season is now fully underway. The typical pattern is clear or overcast mornings, followed by heavy thunderstorms from mid-afternoon. Temperatures actually become slightly more tolerable than April's peak heat because cloud cover and rain cool things down. Most courses stay open and drain well.

**Tee time advice:** Morning tee times (6–9am) are the strategy for the entire rainy season. Most afternoons bring thunder and lightning, which halts play.

**Golf rating: 3/5**

---

### July — Rainy Season

**Temperature:** 26–32°C | **Humidity:** 75–80% | **Rain:** High

July is comfortably warm by the standards of the rainy season. The afternoon thunderstorm rhythm is well-established. Fairways may be lush and soft — ball-striking rewards are high but ground conditions can be muddy in places. Course staff are usually well-practised at managing play around the weather.

**Tee time advice:** Stick to 6–9am. If you book a twilight round, accept that there is a real chance of suspension.

**Golf rating: 3/5**

---

### August — Rainy Season

**Temperature:** 26–32°C | **Humidity:** 75–80% | **Rain:** High

August is similar to July. Afternoon rain is reliable and often heavy. The courses are at their greenest and most lush. August is also low season for tourism, which means better tee-time availability and sometimes promotional green-fee rates.

**Tee time advice:** Morning rounds only. Bring a waterproof jacket in case a storm arrives ahead of schedule.

**Golf rating: 3/5**

---

### September — Rainy Season (Wettest Month)

**Temperature:** 25–32°C | **Humidity:** 75–80% | **Rain:** Very high

September is typically the wettest month of the year in Bangkok. Rain can arrive at any time of day — not just afternoons — and some courses experience temporary waterlogging. That said, determined golfers playing 6am tee times usually complete their rounds without major disruption.

**Tee time advice:** Earliest available tee time. Check course conditions before heading out. If outdoor play does not appeal, this is the top month to book an indoor simulator session.

**Golf rating: 2/5**

---

### October — Rainy Season (Tapering)

**Temperature:** 25–32°C | **Humidity:** 75–80% | **Rain:** High, decreasing

October sees rain beginning to taper off toward the end of the month as the monsoon retreats. Early October can still be very wet. By the final week, some days feel noticeably drier and more like the cool season. A transitional month that rewards flexibility.

**Tee time advice:** Early mornings remain the safest bet through mid-October. By late October, afternoon rounds become viable again.

**Golf rating: 3/5**

---

### November — Cool/Dry Season Begins

**Temperature:** 25–32°C | **Humidity:** Low–Moderate | **Rain:** Low

November is a favourite month for golf tourists. The rains have largely stopped, humidity drops quickly, and temperatures settle into a very comfortable range. Courses recover rapidly from the rainy season and are in excellent playing condition. High-season pricing returns at popular courses as visitor numbers pick up.

**Tee time advice:** All-day play is comfortable. Morning tee times are still pleasant; afternoon rounds are now fully viable.

**Golf rating: 5/5**

---

### December — Cool Season

**Temperature:** 24–31°C | **Humidity:** Low | **Rain:** Very low

December brings Bangkok's most consistently pleasant weather of the year. Temperatures dip to the low-to-mid 20s on cool mornings, peak humidity is modest, and rain is rare. The Christmas and New Year holiday period can create demand for popular courses — book tee times in advance.

**Tee time advice:** Flexible. Early morning is delightful, but afternoon play is equally comfortable.

**Golf rating: 5/5**

## Tee Time Strategy: Morning vs Twilight

**Morning tee times (6–9am)** are the single most effective tool for managing Bangkok's climate year-round. In the hot season they beat the worst of the heat; in the rainy season they beat the afternoon thunderstorms. The majority of Bangkok's 40+ courses within an hour of the city open at or before 6am, and early-bird rates are common.

**Twilight rates** (typically available from around 2–3pm depending on the course) offer good value during the cool season — November through February — when afternoon temperatures are pleasant and afternoon rain is not a concern. During the rainy season (May–October), twilight tee times carry the risk of interruption from thunderstorms, which can result in a suspended or shortened round.

Green fees at Bangkok-area courses generally range from 1,500 THB at municipal tracks up to 5,000 THB or more at premium resort-style venues. Twilight and weekday rates can reduce this significantly. Always confirm current pricing directly with the course before booking.

## Summary: When to Visit for Golf

The clearest recommendation is **November through February** — Bangkok's cool/dry season delivers the best combination of temperature, low humidity, and minimal rain disruption. March and early April are still excellent if you commit to morning tee times. The rainy season from May through October is very manageable with the right approach (early starts, a rain jacket), and course quality is often at its peak due to lush conditions.

There is no month in Bangkok where golf is impossible. There are just months where planning your tee time intelligently makes the difference between a great round and a soggy one.
