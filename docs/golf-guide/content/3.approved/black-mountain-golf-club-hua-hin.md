# Black Mountain Golf Club Hua Hin — Visitor Guide

Black Mountain Golf Club is consistently ranked among the finest golf courses in Asia. Located in Hua Hin, approximately three hours south of Bangkok by car, it draws serious golfers from across the region and has built a reputation for exceptional course design, conditioning, and facilities. If you are planning a golf trip to Thailand and can spare an overnight stay outside Bangkok, Black Mountain deserves to be on your itinerary.

---

## The Course

Black Mountain was designed by **Phil Ryan** and opened in **2007**. The layout spans a dramatic site featuring rocky outcrops, water hazards, and elevation changes that are unusual for courses in this part of Thailand. The design makes full use of the natural terrain — this is not a flat, manufactured course, and the variety between holes keeps the round engaging throughout.

The course has hosted **Asian Tour events** — including the 2009 Black Mountain Masters (won by Johan Edfors) and events in 2010 — and the 2011 Royal Trophy match play between Asia and Europe. It is regularly cited in regional course rankings among the top venues in Southeast Asia. Conditioning is maintained to championship standard, and the attention to detail throughout the property — from the practice facilities to the clubhouse — reflects the premium positioning of the club.

Green fees place Black Mountain at the higher end of the Thai market. Verify current rates directly with the club, as pricing varies by season and tee time, and published third-party figures are often outdated.

---

## Getting There from Bangkok

Hua Hin is approximately **3 hours south of Bangkok** by car, following the main highway south toward the Gulf coast. The drive is straightforward and well-signposted; allow extra time if departing on a Friday afternoon or holiday weekend when southbound traffic can be heavy.

Options for getting there:

1. **Private car or Grab** — most flexible; recommended if you have luggage and clubs
2. **Bus** — regular coaches depart from Bangkok's Southern Bus Terminal (Sai Tai Mai) to Hua Hin; journey time approximately 3–4 hours
3. **Train** — services run from Hua Lamphong station; slower than the bus but scenic; check current schedules
4. **Minivan** — shared minivans run from Victory Monument and other pickup points; faster than the bus but less comfortable with full golf equipment

*Transport times and schedules change — confirm current options before travel.*

## Is It Worth the Trip?

**Yes, for serious golfers.** Black Mountain is not a casual drop-in course. The journey from Bangkok makes it a full-day commitment at minimum, and the course demands genuine engagement — it will test every part of your game.

For golfers who want to play one of the genuinely great courses in Asia, and who are willing to build an overnight trip around it, Black Mountain delivers. Hua Hin itself is a pleasant resort town with good restaurants, hotels at all price points, and beaches that make the non-golf hours enjoyable.

**For casual visitors**, the Bangkok-area courses — Nikanti, Alpine, and others within 45 minutes of the city — offer excellent golf without the travel commitment. See the [best golf courses near Bangkok](/guide/best-golf-courses-near-bangkok) guide for options.

## Practical Information

**Caddies:** Mandatory. Caddie fee typically 300–500 THB. Tip 200–300 THB standard; 300–500 THB for exceptional service.

**Dress code:** Collared shirts required. Tailored shorts acceptable. No cargo shorts, denim, or collarless shirts.

**Green fees:** Premium end of the Thai market — verify current rates directly with Black Mountain. Weekends are higher than weekdays.

**Best time to visit:** November to February (cool season) is ideal. Hua Hin's climate mirrors Bangkok's seasonal patterns, though it can be slightly cooler due to the Gulf breeze. Morning tee times (6–9am) are recommended year-round.

**Booking:** Book directly with the course or through a golf travel platform. Tee times at Black Mountain can book out weeks ahead during peak season — don't leave it to the last minute.

---

## Where to Stay

Hua Hin has accommodation at every level, from budget guesthouses in the town centre to international five-star resorts on the beachfront. The town centre is compact and walkable; most hotels offer golf transfers. An overnight stay is recommended to make the journey worthwhile.
