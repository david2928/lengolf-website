# Nikanti Golf Club Bangkok — Review and Visitor Guide

Nikanti Golf Club has become one of the most talked-about courses in the Bangkok area. Located in Nakhon Pathom province, about 1 hour west of central Bangkok, it offers something genuinely different from the typical Thai parkland layout — a links-style design that is unusual for this part of the world and that draws serious golfers back repeatedly. If you're planning golf in Bangkok, Nikanti belongs on the shortlist.

---

## The Course

Nikanti's defining characteristic is its links-style design. Set on former rice paddies in Nakhon Pathom, the course features elevation changes, undulating fairways, and fast greens unusual for a course in this part of Thailand. It's an 18-hole, par-72 layout structured as three distinct six-hole loops — each comprising two par-3s, two par-4s, and two par-5s — which creates natural flexibility for shorter rounds and a variety of playing sequences.

The course is well-regarded among Bangkok's expat golf community — a group that tends to play regularly and has high standards for course quality and conditioning. Repeat play is common, which is generally a reliable indicator of a course worth visiting.

**Green fees (as of late 2025/early 2026):** 5,500 THB weekday / 6,500 THB weekend. Notably, Nikanti operates an **all-inclusive** model — the fee covers the round, caddie, caddie tip, local beverages, and two meals. There are no additional charges on the day. Verify current rates directly with the course before booking.

---

## Location and Getting There

**Nakhon Pathom, approximately 1 hour west of central Bangkok.**

The most practical options:

1. **Private car or Grab** — recommended; straightforward drive west on the main highway
2. **Golf transfer via hotel concierge** — five-star Bangkok hotels can often arrange shared or private transfers to Nikanti
3. **Public transport** — not practical for golfers with clubs; a car is effectively required

Depart by 5:30am for a 7am tee time to comfortably clear Bangkok's western outskirts. Traffic heading west is lighter than the eastern and northern corridors but can still build during peak hours.

---

## What Makes Nikanti Stand Out

Three things separate Nikanti from many Bangkok-area courses:

1. **Design character** — the links style is rare in Thailand and creates a distinctly different experience from parkland courses. If you've played links courses in the UK or Ireland, you'll find familiar challenges here.
2. **Conditioning** — the course is consistently well-maintained, and the quality holds up across seasons.
3. **Accessible public booking** — unlike some of Bangkok's most prestigious clubs (Thai Country Club, for example), Nikanti welcomes visiting golfers and can be booked online or by phone without needing a member connection.

---

## Practical Information

**Caddies:** Mandatory. Fee typically 300–500 THB. Tip 200–300 THB for a good round; 300–500 THB for exceptional.

**Dress code:** Collared shirt required. Tailored shorts acceptable. No cargo shorts, denim, or collarless shirts.

**Green fees:** 5,500 THB weekday / 6,500 THB weekend (all-inclusive — covers caddie, caddie tip, beverages, and two meals). No surprise extras on the day. Verify current rates before booking.

**Best tee times:** 6–9am year-round. In rainy season (May–October), book the earliest available slot — afternoon thunderstorms are common.

**Booking:** Book directly via the Nikanti website, by phone, or through platforms like GolfNow or GolfAsian. Weekend tee times book out; weekdays are more available.

## Who Is Nikanti Right For?

Nikanti suits golfers who want a genuine challenge and an unusual playing experience. It's not a beginner-friendly resort layout — the open terrain and links character reward experience and penalise erratic ball-striking. Mid-to-low handicappers will get the most out of it, though higher handicappers who want to test themselves on a quality course won't be disappointed.
