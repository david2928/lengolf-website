# Golf in Bangkok During the Rainy Season — What to Expect

If you're planning a golf trip to Bangkok between May and October, the rainy season is probably on your mind. The short answer: yes, you can absolutely play golf — and many visiting golfers do, without their rounds being washed out. The key is understanding how the rain actually behaves here, and adjusting your approach accordingly.

---

## What the Rainy Season Actually Looks Like

Bangkok's rainy season runs from May through October, driven by the southwest monsoon. The name is somewhat misleading — this isn't a season of constant grey skies and all-day drizzle. The typical pattern is clear-to-overcast mornings followed by heavy thunderstorms in the afternoon, usually building from around 1–3pm and clearing by early evening.

Temperatures sit between 25°C and 33°C throughout, so the heat is comparable to the rest of the year. Humidity does climb, reaching 75–80%, with September typically the most oppressive month at around 79%. September is also statistically the wettest month of the season, so if you're especially rain-averse, it's the one to approach with the most flexibility.

The upside of this afternoon-storm pattern is significant for golfers: mornings are usually dry and perfectly playable. Bangkok's courses see regular rounds throughout the wet season precisely because most visitors learn to work with the pattern rather than against it.

---

## Course Conditions During the Wet Months

Bangkok's golf courses are well-maintained and designed with tropical conditions in mind — drainage is generally excellent. After a heavy afternoon downpour, many courses resume play within 30–60 minutes as water moves off greens and fairways quickly. You won't usually face a full day's cancellation from a single storm.

That said, you should expect some modifications to normal playing conditions:

- **Cart path only** is the standard restriction during and after wet periods. Courses enforce this to protect soft fairways from cart traffic. Walking to your ball adds time to your round, so factor that into your schedule.
- **Soft fairways** mean the ball sits up more and won't run as far — adjust your approach shots accordingly.
- **Bunker conditions** can be wet and compacted after rain, affecting sand play in predictable but sometimes tricky ways.
- **Green speeds** may be slightly slower on waterlogged days, though most Bangkok courses manage their greens carefully.

None of these are deal-breakers. They're simply the conditions of wet-season play, and experienced players adapt quickly.

---

## How to Make It Work: The Tee Time Strategy

The single most effective adjustment you can make is booking early morning tee times — 6am or 7am is ideal. This gives you the best chance of completing your full round before the afternoon storms develop. A 7am start typically means you're finishing around 11–11:30am, well ahead of the weather window.

A few practical scheduling tips:

1. **Book early, not late.** Afternoon tee times (2pm+) during rainy season carry a meaningful risk of interruption. If the only slots available are in the afternoon, consider that a reason to look at a different day.
2. **Call ahead on the day.** Bangkok course staff are experienced with wet-season management and will usually give you a reliable read on conditions that morning.
3. **Build flexibility into multi-day itineraries.** If you're visiting for a week, don't schedule golf every single morning — leave a buffer day in case conditions are unusually poor. This removes pressure and lets you play when the weather cooperates.
4. **Check the forecast the evening before.** Thai Meteorological Department forecasts are reasonably reliable at 12–24 hour range for storm likelihood.

## What to Pack

Even with a 7am tee time, you may encounter light rain or high humidity from the moment you arrive at the course. A few additions to your bag will make the difference between a comfortable round and a frustrating one:

1. **Rain glove (or two)** — standard golf gloves become slippery when wet. Rain gloves grip better in wet conditions.
2. **Dry towels** — pack two. One for your grips between shots, one for your hands and face. A waterproof bag clip or carabiner keeps them accessible.
3. **Waterproof golf bag cover** — most caddies carry these, but having your own is never a bad idea.
4. **Lightweight waterproof jacket** — a thin, breathable rain jacket won't overheat you in Bangkok's warmth but keeps you dry during unexpected showers.
5. **Waterproof golf shoes** — if you own them, bring them. If not, dry your shoes between rounds and carry dry socks.
6. **Sunscreen** — humidity and overcast skies are deceptive. UV exposure remains high throughout rainy season.

Leave heavy gear behind. Bangkok's heat means layering is counterproductive; lightweight and quick-drying is the philosophy.

## The Verdict

Rainy season golf in Bangkok is very doable with the right approach. The afternoon thunderstorm pattern means mornings are reliably playable on most days; the courses are well-drained and experienced with wet conditions; and Bangkok's golf infrastructure — caddies, covered clubhouses, cart path protocols — is set up for exactly this. Book 6–7am tee times, carry a rain glove and dry towels, and stay flexible on your afternoon schedule.
