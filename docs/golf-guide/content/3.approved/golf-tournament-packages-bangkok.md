# Golf Tournament Packages in Bangkok

Bangkok is one of Southeast Asia's most practical cities for organising a golf tournament. Courses within the metropolitan area range from championship-grade layouts to accessible resort tracks, green fees remain reasonable by international standards, and the city's hospitality infrastructure — transfers, catering, prize-sourcing — is mature enough to support events of any size. Whether you are running a corporate day for 20 colleagues, a society competition for 60 members, or a smaller group wanting a competitive day out, Bangkok can accommodate it. For groups that prefer to avoid the logistics of a course day entirely, [LENGOLF](/golf) offers a fully indoor, simulator-based tournament format in a central location.

---

## 1. Real-Course Tournaments in Bangkok

Bangkok-area courses regularly host visitor and society tournaments. Most will take groups of 8 to 80+ players, and the larger clubs have dedicated events teams who can manage tee-time blocking, scorecards, and on-course coordination on your behalf.

**Common formats for group days:**

1. Strokeplay — gross or nett, the most straightforward to administer
2. Stableford — popular for mixed-handicap groups; it keeps all players engaged throughout
3. Scramble / Texas Scramble — best for corporate events where not everyone is a strong golfer
4. 2-ball or 4-ball betterball — works well for smaller groups of 8–16
5. Skins — adds side-game interest without changing the primary competition structure

**What courses typically provide for tournament days:**

1. A reserved block of consecutive tee times (usually shotgun or rolling start)
2. Scorecards and rules sheets
3. On-course marshalling
4. A dedicated area for a prize presentation or post-round meal
5. Optional buggy hire, caddie allocation, and range tokens

Booking requires more lead time than a standard tee time — contact the course's events coordinator at least four to six weeks in advance for groups over 20, and two to three weeks for smaller parties. See the [corporate golf events Bangkok guide](/guide/corporate-golf-events-bangkok) for a full breakdown of venue options and pricing tiers.

## 3. Planning Checklist

Use this checklist when organising either a real-course or simulator tournament in Bangkok:

1. Confirm the group size and date range at least four weeks before the event
2. Decide on format (strokeplay, Stableford, scramble, simulator-based) before approaching venues
3. Book the venue and tee times (or simulator bays) and confirm the block in writing
4. Collect player information: names, handicaps if applicable, dietary requirements for catering
5. Arrange transport — shared transfers from a central point simplify logistics considerably
6. Source prizes at least two weeks out (see Section 5 below)
7. Prepare scorecards, rules sheet, and any tie-breaker criteria
8. Brief a rules coordinator or designate an event MC for the prize presentation
9. Confirm catering arrangements and post-round venue at least one week before

## 4. Scoring and Prizes

For real-course events, the course pro shop or events team can usually provide official scorecards and submit results to a local handicap system if required. For informal society events, a simple spreadsheet shared on the day is sufficient.

For simulator events at LENGOLF, scores are recorded digitally within the simulator platform, which makes aggregation fast and removes scorecard disputes entirely.

Prizes do not need to be expensive to be well-received. A common structure for Bangkok tournaments:

1. First, second, and third overall
2. Nearest the pin (one hole designated — or on the simulator, the closest-to-pin format itself)
3. Longest drive
4. Best front nine / best back nine
5. Last place award — keeps the tone light for corporate or society days

Branded prizes sourced locally (golf accessories, clothing, LENGOLF vouchers) tend to travel better than trophies for international groups.

## 5. Catering and Post-Tournament

Most Bangkok courses have a clubhouse restaurant that can host a sit-down meal or buffet for your group after the round. Agreeing on a set menu in advance simplifies billing and avoids long waits. For simulator events at LENGOLF, the facility's layout supports food and beverage service alongside play, so eating and competing are not mutually exclusive.

Post-tournament functions work best when the prize presentation is scheduled as part of the meal rather than a separate event — it keeps the group together and maintains momentum after a long day on the course.

## 6. Key Questions to Ask When Booking

Before confirming any tournament booking — course or simulator — ask the following:

1. What is the minimum and maximum group size for a tournament day?
2. Is there a dedicated events coordinator, and who is the main contact on the day?
3. What is included in the base price (caddies, buggies, scorecards, range tokens)?
4. What is the cancellation and weather policy?
5. Can the venue handle prize presentation and catering in-house, or do you need to arrange externally?
6. Are there any restrictions on outside prizes, alcohol, or branded materials?
7. For simulator bookings: can multiple bays run simultaneously to accommodate the group size?
