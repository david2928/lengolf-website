# Best Airlines to Fly with Golf Clubs to Bangkok

Choosing the right airline for your Bangkok golf trip isn't just about ticket price. Baggage allowances, excess fee structures, and route connections all affect your total cost — particularly when travelling with a golf bag.

The good news: every major full-service airline includes your golf bag within your standard checked allowance. No separate golf surcharge. The question is which airlines have the most generous allowances and the most forgiving excess fee structure if you go slightly over.

**Note:** Airline policies change. Always confirm current fees and allowances directly with your carrier before booking.

---

## What Makes an Airline Good for Golf Travel

A good airline for a Bangkok golf trip offers:

1. **Generous weight or piece allowance** — enough to cover clubs, travel bag, and shoes without going over
2. **Golfer-friendly excess structure** — if you do go over, how much does it cost?
3. **Convenient routing** — fewer connections means less handling risk for your clubs
4. **Good flight frequency** — flexibility to choose times that suit you

---

## Top Airlines for Golf Travel to Bangkok

### 1. Thai Airways
**Best for:** Golfers flying from Asia; most golf-friendly standard policy

Thai Airways has one of the clearest golf-friendly policies among carriers to Bangkok. Golf equipment (up to 14 clubs, 12 balls, one pair of shoes) counts as one free checked piece within your standard allowance — no separate fee, no golf surcharge.

- **Allowance**: Piece concept (effective 2 March 2026) — one free piece in economy on most routes
- **If over allowance**: Flat rate per piece — USD 150/unit on USA/Canada routes; zone-specific on other international routes
- **Key routes**: Bangkok direct from multiple Asian cities; long-haul via partner connections
- **Verdict**: Strong first choice — clear policy, no hidden golf fees

### 2. Singapore Airlines
**Best for:** Australian and New Zealand golfers; best excess fee structure

Singapore Airlines flies via Singapore and consistently rates among the best full-service carriers globally. For golfers, the standout feature is the excess baggage concession: if your golf bag pushes you over your allowance, you are charged at a flat **6 kg rate** (up to 15 kg of excess) rather than the full weight of the bag. This is the most forgiving excess structure among major carriers.

- **Allowance**: Economy 25–30 kg depending on route and fare class
- **Golf concession**: Flat 6 kg charge if over allowance (up to 15 kg excess); standard rates apply beyond that
- **One set**: Max 14 clubs + 12 balls + 1 pair shoes + tees, in a protective bag or case
- **Key routes**: Sydney, Melbourne, Auckland, Perth → Singapore → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: Best choice if you expect to be marginally over allowance — the 6 kg concession is a meaningful saving

### 3. Cathay Pacific
**Best for:** Australian, UK, and North American golfers; same 6 kg concession as SQ

Cathay Pacific routes through Hong Kong with solid connectivity from Australia, the UK, and North America. Crucially, Cathay has the same golfer-friendly excess structure as Singapore Airlines: if the golf bag causes you to exceed your allowance, you are charged at a flat **6 kg rate** (up to 15 kg excess) rather than the full bag weight.

- **Allowance**: Economy typically 1 piece at 23 kg (piece concept on most routes)
- **Golf concession**: Same 6 kg flat rate structure as Singapore Airlines
- **Key routes**: Sydney, London, New York, Vancouver → Hong Kong → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: Strong choice from Australia, UK, and North America — the 6 kg concession makes going slightly over affordable

### 4. Emirates
**Best for:** European, Middle Eastern, and African golfers; excellent business class

Emirates operates via Dubai with one of the broadest global networks. Golf bags travel within your standard allowance — no separate golf fee. If you exceed your weight allowance, standard excess baggage rates apply (route-dependent per kg).

- **Allowance**: Economy 20–35 kg depending on route and fare (weight concept)
- **If over allowance**: Standard excess baggage rates — no golf-specific concession
- **Key routes**: London, Paris, Frankfurt, Johannesburg, Mumbai → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: Excellent from Europe and Africa; business class allowances easily cover most golf setups

### 5. Qatar Airways
**Best for:** European, South Asian, and African golfers; 30 kg economy allowance

Qatar flies via Doha with extensive global coverage. Golf bags travel within the standard allowance; excess charges are standard rates if over. Qatar is notable for offering additional baggage purchases online at a discount (up to 20% off if purchased 6+ hours before departure).

- **Allowance**: Economy typically 30 kg on most international routes (fare-dependent)
- **If over allowance**: Standard excess rates — discounted if purchased online in advance
- **Key routes**: London, Dublin, Paris, Cape Town, Mumbai, Colombo → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: 30 kg economy allowance is one of the more generous on long-haul routes

## Airlines to Approach Carefully

### Budget Carriers (AirAsia, Nok Air, Scoot)

Budget carriers offer low base fares but no included checked baggage. Golf bags must be added as a paid item at booking — airport rates are significantly higher. The "cheap" base fare often ends up comparable to or more expensive than full-service tickets once the golf bag fee, seat selection, and meals are added.

Budget carriers make most sense for short regional hops (intra-Asia connections) where the golf bag fee is lower and the flight duration is short.

## Tips for Choosing Your Airline

1. **Calculate total trip cost** — ticket + potential excess (return) — not just the headline fare
2. **Choose SQ or Cathay if you're likely to be over allowance** — the 6 kg concession is the best structure available
3. **Check your loyalty status** — elite tier on most carriers waives excess fees entirely
4. **Weigh your packed bag before travel** — if under the allowance, all major carriers fly your clubs for free
