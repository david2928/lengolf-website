# Golf Club Baggage Fees — Every Major Airline to Bangkok Compared

Flying to Bangkok with your golf clubs? The good news: every major full-service airline includes your golf bag within your standard checked baggage allowance — there is no separate "sporting equipment surcharge" on top of your ticket.

The real cost risk is going over your weight allowance. This guide explains how each airline handles golf bags, what excess charges look like, and how to avoid paying anything extra.

**Important:** Airline baggage policies change. Always verify current fees and allowances directly with your airline before booking. Figures below reflect policies as of early 2026.

---

## How Airlines Actually Treat Golf Bags

A common misconception is that airlines charge a flat "golf equipment fee" on top of your normal baggage. On major full-service carriers flying to Bangkok, this is not how it works.

The standard approach:

- Your golf bag counts as **one of your checked baggage pieces**, using your existing free allowance
- No separate sporting equipment surcharge applies if you stay within your weight/piece limit
- If your golf bag pushes your total checked weight over the free allowance, **standard excess baggage rates** apply — the same per-kg or per-piece rates that apply to any overweight luggage

The practical implication: if you pack light and stay under your allowance, flying with golf clubs costs nothing extra. The weight of your travel bag and clubs is what to watch.

Budget carriers are different — see below.

## Golf Bag Allowances by Airline

### Thai Airways

Thai Airways is one of the most golf-friendly carriers to Bangkok. Golf equipment (up to 14 clubs, 12 balls, one pair of shoes) counts as one free checked piece within your standard allowance.

- **Piece allowance**: One free piece in economy on most routes (Thai Airways moved to piece concept on **2 March 2026**)
- **If over allowance**: Flat rate applies per piece — **USD 150 per unit** on USA/Canada routes; other international zones are zone-specific (check thaiairways.com for your route)
- **Key routes**: Intra-Asia, Tokyo, Seoul, Sydney, and long-haul via partner connections
- **Verdict**: Strong first choice from Asia — generous allowance, no golf surcharge

### Emirates

Emirates operates via Dubai with strong connectivity from Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Asia.

- **Weight allowance**: Economy 20–35 kg depending on route and fare; golf bag weight counts toward this total
- **If over allowance**: Standard excess baggage rates apply (per kg, route-dependent) — no dedicated golf tariff
- **Key routes**: London, Paris, Frankfurt, Johannesburg, Mumbai → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: Excellent for business class where generous allowances easily cover golf bags; economy passengers should weigh their bag before travel

### Qatar Airways

Qatar flies via Doha with one of the largest global networks.

- **Weight allowance**: Economy typically 30 kg on most international routes (fare-dependent); golf bag weight counts toward total
- **If over allowance**: Standard excess baggage rates apply — no separate golf fee. Qatar offers up to 20% discount on additional baggage purchased online at least 6 hours before departure
- **Key routes**: London, Dublin, Paris, Cape Town, Mumbai, Colombo → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: Reliable choice; clear policy documentation on their website

### Singapore Airlines

Singapore Airlines via Singapore is a top-rated full-service carrier, particularly popular from Australia and New Zealand.

- **Weight allowance**: Economy 25–30 kg depending on route and fare; golf bag weight counts toward total
- **Golfer-friendly concession**: If the golf bag pushes your total over the free allowance, Singapore Airlines charges you at a flat **6 kg rate** (up to a maximum of 15 kg excess for the golf bag) rather than the full bag weight. Beyond 15 kg excess, normal excess rates apply.
- One set = max 14 clubs + 12 balls + 1 pair golf shoes + tees in a protective bag or case
- **Key routes**: Sydney, Melbourne, Auckland, Perth → Singapore → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: The 6 kg concession is a meaningful saving for anyone slightly over — one of the best policies for golfers

### Cathay Pacific

Cathay Pacific operates via Hong Kong with strong links from Australia, the UK, and North America.

- **Piece allowance**: Economy typically 1 piece at 23 kg on most routes (piece concept)
- **Golfer-friendly concession**: Same structure as Singapore Airlines — if the golf bag causes you to exceed your allowance, you are charged at a flat **6 kg rate** (up to 15 kg excess) rather than the full bag weight
- One set = golf bag containing clubs + 1 pair golf shoes
- **Key routes**: Sydney, London, New York, Vancouver → Hong Kong → Bangkok
- **Verdict**: The 6 kg concession makes Cathay a strong choice for golfers who might be marginally over allowance

## Budget Carriers (AirAsia, Nok Air, Scoot)

Budget carriers flying to Bangkok typically **do not include any checked baggage in the base fare**. Golf bags must be added as a paid item at booking — airport walk-up rates are significantly higher.

- Golf bag fee varies by carrier and route — add at booking to get the lower rate
- Calculate the total cost (base fare + golf bag fee + seat selection + meals) before assuming the budget fare is cheaper than a full-service ticket
- Budget carriers make more sense for short regional hops (e.g. intra-Asia connections) where the golf bag fee is lower and the flight is short

## What Drives Your Total Cost

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Travel class | Business/first allowances almost always cover golf bags without excess |
| Total packed weight | The single biggest variable — weigh before you go |
| Airline choice | SQ and Cathay's 6 kg concession is best if slightly over allowance |
| Loyalty status | Elite status on most carriers waives excess charges entirely |
| Fare class within economy | Full-fare economy (Y/B class) often has higher allowances than discounted fares |

## Tips to Keep Costs Low

1. **Use a lightweight soft travel bag** — saves 4–7 kg vs a hard case, keeping you within allowance on more airlines
2. **Pack golf balls in your main suitcase** — a full bag of balls adds 2 kg to your golf bag unnecessarily
3. **Choose Singapore Airlines or Cathay Pacific** if you expect to be marginally over — the 6 kg concession is the most forgiving structure
4. **Check your loyalty status** — elite tier on most carriers waives excess fees entirely
5. **Consider renting clubs in Bangkok** — for short trips, [renting at LENGOLF](/golf-club-rental) or at your course may cost less than two-way excess charges. See: [Should you bring clubs or rent?](/guide/bring-golf-clubs-thailand-or-rent)

## Key Takeaways

- Major airlines do **not** charge a separate upfront golf equipment fee — the bag travels within your standard allowance
- The cost risk is exceeding your weight allowance, not a golf surcharge
- **Singapore Airlines and Cathay Pacific** offer a golfer-friendly 6 kg flat rate concession if you go over
- **Thai Airways** is a strong choice from Asia — golf equipment counted as one free piece
- Budget carriers charge separately — always calculate the total before booking
- Always verify current policies with your airline before travel — fees and allowances change
