# Alpine Golf Club Bangkok — Visitor Guide

Alpine Golf Club is one of Bangkok's most established championship courses. Located in Pathum Thani province, about 30 minutes north of the city, it has a pedigree that few Bangkok-area courses can match — it has hosted events on the Asian Tour, a fact that speaks to the quality of the layout and the standard of conditioning required to do so. For golfers looking for a serious round close to the capital, Alpine is a natural choice.

---

## The Course

Alpine is an 18-hole, par-72 championship layout stretching 7,100 yards, built in 1996. The traditional parkland character — mature trees lining the fairways, strategic water features, and greens that require precise approach play — gives the course a classic, settled feel.

The course's most notable achievement is hosting the **2000 Johnnie Walker Classic**, an Asian Tour event won by Tiger Woods. That pedigree sets expectations appropriately: the layout has been tested by tour professionals and maintained to standards required for elite competition. For visiting amateurs, that heritage translates to a challenging, fair layout that rewards solid ball-striking and patient course management.

**Green fees (as of late 2025):** approximately 5,400 THB weekday / 7,400 THB weekend (inclusive of caddie and cart). Verify current rates directly with Alpine before booking.

Green fees are in the mid-to-premium range for Bangkok-area courses. Verify current rates directly with Alpine — pricing varies by season and day of week, and third-party platforms don't always reflect the latest figures.

## Location and Getting There

**Pathum Thani, approximately 30 minutes north of central Bangkok.**

The northern route is one of the easier motorway corridors out of Bangkok — less congested than routes east or west during peak hours. Getting there:

1. **Private car or Grab** — the practical choice; 30 minutes in normal traffic
2. **Hotel concierge transfer** — many Bangkok hotels offer golf transfer arrangements to nearby courses including Alpine
3. **Public transport** — not practical with clubs; a car is required

For a 7am tee time, departing Bangkok by 6am is comfortable. The route is straightforward and well-marked.

---

## What to Expect on the Day

**Caddies:** Mandatory. Fee typically 300–500 THB, paid to the course. Tip 200–300 THB for a good round; 300–500 THB for exceptional service.

**Dress code:** Collared shirt required. Tailored shorts acceptable. No cargo shorts, denim, or collarless shirts.

**Green fees:** Mid-to-premium for Bangkok. Weekends higher than weekdays. Confirm current rates directly with Alpine before booking.

**Best tee times:** 6–9am year-round. Aim for the earliest available slot during rainy season (May–October) to finish before afternoon storms.

**Booking:** Book directly via the course website, by phone, or through an established golf booking platform. Weekday tee times are more available; weekends at a prestige course like Alpine fill up.

## Alpine vs. Nikanti — How Do They Compare?

Both Alpine and Nikanti are among the best accessible courses near Bangkok, and serious Bangkok golfers will eventually play both. The key difference is design character:

- **Alpine** — traditional parkland; tree-lined, classic layout; rewards accuracy and course management
- **Nikanti** — links-style; more open, wind-exposed, unusual for Thailand; rewards trajectory control

If you can only play one, your preference for course style is the deciding factor. If you have multiple days in Bangkok, play both — they offer genuinely different experiences.

## Who Is Alpine Right For?

Alpine suits golfers who want a proper championship test near Bangkok without the logistics of a Hua Hin trip or the access challenges of a private club. The Asian Tour heritage sets expectations appropriately — this is a course that demands solid ball-striking and patient course management. Suitable for mid-handicappers and above; lower handicappers will relish the challenge.
