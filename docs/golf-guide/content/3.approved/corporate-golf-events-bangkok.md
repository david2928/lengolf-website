# Corporate Golf Events in Bangkok — Complete Planning Guide

Bangkok has a well-established corporate golf culture. Companies use golf days for client entertainment, team-building, and incentive programmes — and the city's golf infrastructure, spanning full championship courses on the outskirts and indoor simulator venues in the centre, means there are realistic options for every brief and budget.

This guide covers what is involved in each format, how to plan for mixed-ability groups, and what to consider around logistics, catering, and lead times. For broader context on golf in the city, see the [Golf in Thailand Guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide).

---

## 1. Outdoor Course Events — What Is Actually Involved

A corporate day at a Bangkok-area golf course is a significant production. The city's best-regarded courses — Nikanti, Rachakram, Alpine, and others covered in our [guide to the best courses near Bangkok](/guide/best-golf-courses-near-bangkok) — offer corporate packages that typically include:

- Reserved tee blocks or a shotgun start for the whole group
- Caddie assignment for every player
- Buggies or cart allocation
- Lunch or dinner at the clubhouse
- Scoring, prizes, and occasionally on-course games or closest-to-pin competitions

The experience, done well, is excellent. But the logistics are considerable.

**Practical realities of an outdoor course day:**

1. **Half-day travel commitment on each end.** Bangkok's best corporate-grade courses are 45–90 minutes from the CBD. Accounting for the drive in each direction, a full round, and a post-round meal, you are looking at a 9–12 hour commitment for participants.
2. **Weather dependency.** Bangkok's wet season runs from May through October. Afternoon thunderstorms are common and can suspend play. Full cancellation due to weather is rare, but disrupted rounds and uncomfortable conditions are not.
3. **Dress code and equipment requirements.** Collared shirts, appropriate footwear, and no denim are standard requirements. Participants without clubs need to hire them in advance, which requires coordination with the course pro shop.
4. **Caddie logistics.** Every player will be assigned a caddie. This is standard practice in Thailand, and most corporate guests unfamiliar with the system will need a briefing beforehand.
5. **Extended lead time.** Reserving a full tee block or shotgun start at a well-regarded course typically requires booking 2–4 weeks in advance, more during peak season (November–February).

For companies entertaining clients who play regularly, or for incentive trips where the course experience itself is the centrepiece, the outdoor format is the right choice. For internal team events or situations where attendance includes non-golfers and beginners, the logistics can create friction.

## 3. Planning Checklist for a Bangkok Corporate Golf Day

Whether you choose an outdoor course or an indoor venue, the following checklist covers the key planning items.

1. **Define the objective.** Client entertainment, internal team-building, and incentive events each have different requirements for exclusivity, duration, and polish.
2. **Confirm participant count and ability range.** The ratio of experienced golfers to beginners shapes the format, scoring system, and venue choice.
3. **Set the budget per head.** Outdoor course events in Bangkok typically range from 3,000–8,000 THB per person including green fees, caddies, and a meal. Indoor simulator packages vary; contact the venue directly for group rates.
4. **Decide on format.** Strokeplay, Stableford, Texas Scramble, and nearest-to-pin games each suit different group dynamics. Scramble formats work well for mixed-ability groups at outdoor courses; simulator-based competitions offer flexibility to customise scoring.
5. **Book in advance.** For outdoor courses, 3–4 weeks minimum. For indoor venues, 1–2 weeks is usually sufficient for small groups, though larger events benefit from earlier notice.
6. **Arrange equipment for non-owners.** Confirm club hire availability when booking, not on the day.
7. **Brief participants.** Send a pre-event note covering dress code, start time, travel logistics, and what is included. First-time players at Thai courses will benefit from a brief note on caddie etiquette.
8. **Plan the scoring and prizes.** Even a light prize structure (nearest the pin, longest drive, team winner) improves engagement significantly. Order anything physical — vouchers, branded items, trophies — at least a week out.
9. **Confirm catering.** Whether lunch at a clubhouse or post-round drinks at the simulator venue, food and beverage logistics should be confirmed before the event, not handled on the day.
10. **Designate an on-the-day coordinator.** Someone needs to own the running order, manage latecomers, keep scoring, and liaise with venue staff. This should not also be the most senior person in the room.

## 4. Mixed-Ability Groups — How to Handle Them

Corporate golf events almost always involve a range of abilities. Managing this well is the difference between an event that energises a group and one that leaves beginners embarrassed and experienced players bored.

**For outdoor course events:**

- Use a **Texas Scramble** format, where every player hits and the group plays from the best shot. This levels the field, keeps pace, and means beginners contribute without holding up play.
- Pair beginners with experienced players rather than grouping them together.
- Consider a 9-hole format rather than 18 if the group includes several non-regular golfers.

**For indoor simulator events:**

- The software on most simulator platforms allows you to set different tee positions and course difficulties per player, so experienced golfers and beginners can play the same course on different settings.
- Stableford scoring with an applied handicap keeps competition genuine across the range.
- Because the format is inherently more casual, mixed-ability groups tend to be self-managing — players pace themselves without the pressure of holding up a fairway.

For any corporate group, avoid stroke play with full scoring transparency when ability ranges are wide. Public scorecards highlighting a beginner's 110 against a 15-handicapper's 85 serves no one.

## 5. Catering and Post-Golf Entertainment

**Outdoor courses:** Most Bangkok-area courses with corporate packages have full restaurant facilities. A post-round meal at the clubhouse is standard, and course staff are generally experienced at running prize presentations in this setting. Confirm whether the package includes drinks or whether these are charged additionally — both arrangements exist.

**Indoor venues:** Post-round catering depends on the venue. LENGOLF can accommodate food and beverage for groups — confirm specifics when booking. For larger events, the simulator session can be paired with a separate dinner reservation nearby, with the venue acting as the pre-dinner activity rather than the full event.

**Evening format:** If the corporate event is intended to serve as client entertainment rather than a stand-alone team activity, structuring it as a 2-hour simulator session followed by dinner works well. The golf provides a structured activity and talking point; dinner allows the relationship-building to continue without time pressure.
