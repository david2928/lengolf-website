# Best Golf Courses Near Bangkok — Complete Guide

Bangkok is one of the best cities in the world for a golfer to be based. With 50+ courses within roughly an hour of the city centre, you could play a different course every day for several weeks without repeating. Prices are a fraction of equivalent courses in Europe, Japan, or Australia. Caddies are included. And tee times are available year-round. Here's everything you need to know to make the most of it.

---

## How to Choose a Course

Before booking, narrow down by four factors:

1. **Location from your hotel** — Bangkok's traffic is significant. A course 40km away can take 90 minutes in peak hour. Check the direction from your hotel and plan accordingly.
2. **Budget** — Green fees range from around 1,500 THB at accessible daily-fee courses to 5,000+ THB at premium clubs. Weekends cost 20–40% more.
3. **Skill level and style** — Some courses are genuine championship challenges; others are resort-friendly and forgiving. Know what kind of round you want.
4. **Public access** — Most Bangkok-area courses accept visiting golfers. A few operate primarily for members and require advance arrangement or a contact.

## Notable Courses Near Bangkok

### Nikanti Golf Club — Nakhon Pathom (~45 min west)

Nikanti is one of the most talked-about courses in the Bangkok area and a firm favourite among the city's expat golf community. Its links-style design is unusual for Thailand — open fairways, undulating terrain, and wind that plays a genuine role — and the conditioning is consistently high. It's accessible to visiting golfers and bookable online, making it an ideal first Bangkok course for international visitors.

### Alpine Golf Club — Pathum Thani (~30 min north)

Alpine has Asian Tour heritage and the course quality to match. Located north of Bangkok in Pathum Thani, it's one of the easier courses to reach from the city without fighting eastbound or westbound motorway traffic. The layout is mature, tree-lined, and offers a classic parkland experience. A good option for serious golfers who want a proper challenge.

### Thai Country Club — Chonburi (~1 hour east)

One of the most prestigious clubs in Thailand, Thai Country Club operates primarily as a private members' club. Public access is limited — visiting golfers typically need a hotel concierge connection, a member invitation, or direct advance arrangement with the club. For those who can get in, it represents one of the finest rounds available in the country. See our [Thai Country Club visitor guide](/guide/thai-country-club-bangkok) for full details.

### Hua Hin (day trip / overnight)

Black Mountain Golf Club in Hua Hin, about three hours south of Bangkok, is regularly cited among the best courses in Asia. It's not a day-trip course for casual golfers — the drive requires an overnight stay to make it worthwhile — but for serious golfers building a Thailand golf itinerary, it belongs on the list. See our [Black Mountain visitor guide](/guide/black-mountain-golf-club-hua-hin) for full details.

## What to Expect at Thai Golf Courses

### Caddies

Caddies are **mandatory** at virtually all Bangkok-area courses. They carry your bag, clean clubs, read greens, and provide yardage. The caddie fee is typically **300–500 THB**, paid to the course separately from your green fee. A tip of **200–300 THB** is standard for good service; **300–500 THB** for an exceptional round.

Don't underestimate how much a good caddie improves your round. Local knowledge of slopes, pin positions, and prevailing winds can easily save 3–5 strokes on an unfamiliar course.

### Dress Code

Collared shirts are required at all courses without exception. Tailored shorts are generally acceptable. Cargo shorts, denim, football shirts, and sleeveless tops are not. Most pro shops sell basics if you've forgotten something.

### Booking

Most Bangkok-area courses can be booked directly by phone or email, or through online platforms such as GolfNow, GolfAsian, or GolfSavers. Weekdays are easier to secure; weekends book out faster, particularly at popular courses. Book at least a few days ahead to secure your preferred tee time.

| Category | Typical weekday range | Typical weekend range |
|----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Accessible daily-fee courses | 1,500–2,500 THB | 2,000–3,500 THB |
| Mid-range championship courses | 2,500–3,500 THB | 3,000–4,500 THB |
| Premium / prestige courses | 3,500–5,000+ THB | 4,500–6,000+ THB |

Fees above are indicative and do not include caddie fees or buggy hire. Always confirm current rates directly with the course — prices change seasonally and are sometimes not updated on third-party platforms.

## Best Time to Play

**November to February** is the ideal season — cool, dry, and comfortable. Temperatures hover around 25–32°C with low humidity. Morning tee times (6–9am) are ideal year-round; in peak season they're essential.

**March to May** is the hot season — temperatures can reach 38–40°C by mid-morning. Start as early as possible (6–7am) and consider finishing nine holes rather than pushing through 18 in full heat.

**May to October** is rainy season. Afternoon thunderstorms are common, but mornings are usually clear. Many serious golfers prefer this window for the lower green fees and quieter courses.
