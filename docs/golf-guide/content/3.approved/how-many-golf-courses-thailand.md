# How Many Golf Courses Are There in Thailand?

## The Short Answer

Thailand is home to approximately 250–300 golf courses, though figures vary by source depending on whether private, resort, and military courses are all counted. Of these, around 100–150 are considered suitable for international visitors. What is certain is that the country ranks among Asia's most golf-dense destinations, with a well-developed network of courses spanning every major region. Bangkok alone has more than 40 courses within an hour of the city centre — a figure that gives you a sense of the scale on offer nationwide.

## Regional Breakdown

Thailand's golf courses are concentrated in five main areas, each with its own character:

1. **Bangkok and Central Thailand** — The largest concentration in the country, with 50+ courses within roughly one hour of central Bangkok. Courses range from premium championship layouts to accessible daily-fee venues, making the capital the natural starting point for most golf-focused visitors.

2. **Hua Hin and Pranburi** — A significant concentration of courses lines the Gulf coast south of Bangkok. Many of the country's most celebrated layouts are found here, drawing weekend golfers from the capital and international visitors alike.

3. **Phuket and the Andaman Coast** — Phuket hosts a meaningful cluster of courses, typically set against dramatic hillside scenery. Course quality trends upmarket, reflecting the island's premium tourism positioning.

4. **Chiang Mai and Northern Thailand** — A growing concentration of courses sits in and around Chiang Mai, benefiting from cooler highland temperatures. Course conditions here are often excellent during the cool season (November–February).

5. **Pattaya and the Eastern Seaboard** — The Eastern Seaboard has accumulated a substantial roster of courses, historically popular with expat golfers and weekend visitors from Bangkok.

## Why Thailand Has So Many Courses

Several factors converged to make Thailand a golf powerhouse:

1. **Favorable climate** — With sunshine year-round and no frost, courses can operate continuously and conditions rarely suffer the seasonal closures common in temperate countries.
2. **Strong expat and tourism base** — A large residential expat community, combined with millions of annual visitors from Japan, South Korea, China, and Europe, sustains year-round demand.
3. **Golf culture in the region** — Golf carries significant social and business cachet across East and Southeast Asia, and Thailand has been a regional hub for the sport since at least the 1990s.
4. **Relatively low development and operating costs** — Land availability outside city centres and competitive labour costs have historically made course development financially viable.

## What This Means for Visitors

In practical terms, the sheer number of courses means visitors are genuinely spoiled for choice. Budget-conscious golfers can find quality daily-fee courses for under 1,000 THB; those seeking a premium experience will find championship venues with immaculate conditioning at a fraction of what they would pay in Europe or North America. Tee-time availability is rarely a problem outside peak holiday weekends, and most courses welcome walk-in bookings.
