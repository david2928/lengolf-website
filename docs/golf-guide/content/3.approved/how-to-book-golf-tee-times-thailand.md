# How to Book Golf Tee Times in Thailand

Booking a round of golf in Thailand is far simpler than many visitors expect. With more than 50 courses within an hour of Bangkok's city centre alone, the main challenge is choosing where to play — not navigating a complicated reservation system. Whether you prefer to book in advance from home or arrange something the morning you arrive, there is an option that fits how you travel.

---

## Three Ways to Book a Tee Time

### 1. Book Directly with the Course

The most straightforward method is contacting the course by phone or email. Almost every golf club in Thailand accepts direct reservations, and staff at the larger clubs typically speak enough English to handle a booking without difficulty. The course's own website usually lists a contact number and, increasingly, a simple web form.

Direct booking works well when you already know which course you want, when you are a returning visitor, or when you are planning a group round and need to discuss logistics. You will often get the most accurate information about current green fees, promotional rates, and any temporary course conditions straight from the source.

### 2. Use an Online Booking Platform

Several platforms aggregate tee times across Thai golf courses and let you compare availability and pricing in one place. Commonly used options include GolfAsian, GolfNow, GolfSavers, and Deemples. These platforms can be convenient if you are still deciding between courses or if you want to browse options across a region before committing.

As with any third-party booking service, it is worth reading the cancellation and amendment policy carefully before you confirm. Availability shown online is generally reliable, but confirming your booking reference with the course directly — especially for weekend rounds — adds a useful layer of certainty.

### 3. Ask Your Hotel Concierge

If you are staying at a four- or five-star hotel in Bangkok, the concierge desk is a genuinely useful resource for arranging golf. Concierge teams at the larger hotels maintain relationships with nearby clubs, can handle the phone call on your behalf, and will know which courses are currently in good condition. For visitors who prefer not to deal with logistics themselves, this remains one of the easiest ways to secure a tee time, particularly at short notice.

---

## How Far in Advance Should You Book?

On weekdays, many courses will accommodate same-day or next-day requests, especially outside of public holidays. For weekend rounds, aim to book at least three to five days ahead — Bangkok-area courses fill up quickly on Saturdays and Sundays, with early morning slots going first.

Peak season runs from roughly December through February, when cooler temperatures and lower humidity make conditions ideal. During these months, popular courses can be fully booked a week or more in advance. If your travel dates fall in peak season and you have a specific course in mind, booking before you leave home is a sensible precaution.

---

## Tee Time Strategy: Morning vs Twilight

The best tee times year-round are between 6 am and 9 am. Starting early means cooler temperatures, lower humidity, and courses that are rarely busy. An early finish also leaves the afternoon free.

Many courses offer twilight rates for rounds beginning after 2 pm or 3 pm. These discounted slots are worth considering if you are flexible on timing and want to keep costs down — green fees are typically lower than standard rates, and you will often find more availability. Bear in mind that twilight rounds may not allow you to complete all 18 holes if daylight runs short, so check the expected finish time when booking.

Green fees across the Bangkok region range from approximately 1,500 THB at more modest layouts to 5,000 THB or more at premium clubs. Weekend rates commonly run 20 to 40 percent higher than weekday pricing.

---

## What to Confirm When Booking

Before you finalise any reservation, get clear answers on the following:

1. **Green fee** — confirm the exact rate for your day and start time, including any weekend or peak-season surcharge.
2. **Caddie fee** — caddies are mandatory at virtually all Thai golf courses. The fee is separate from the green fee and typically falls between 300 and 500 THB, paid directly to the caddie. Tips are customary.
3. **Buggy availability** — electric buggies are available at most courses but may carry an additional hire charge. Confirm whether walking is permitted if you prefer it.
4. **Dress code** — collared shirts are standard. Some clubs prohibit denim or shorts above the knee. A quick check before you pack saves embarrassment at the starter's hut.
5. **Cancellation policy** — understand the cut-off for changes, particularly if your itinerary is subject to weather or schedule shifts.

## Day-of Tips

Arrive at the course at least 30 minutes before your tee time. This gives you time to check in, settle any pre-payment required, collect your caddie assignment, and warm up on the range if one is available.

Payment is usually handled at the pro shop on arrival. Most clubs accept cash in Thai baht; credit card acceptance varies, so it is worth carrying sufficient cash as a backup. Caddie fees and tips are almost always cash only.

Bring plenty of water or purchase it at the clubhouse — even in the cooler months, heat and humidity on a Thai course can be significant. Sunscreen, a hat, and a spare glove are worth packing.
