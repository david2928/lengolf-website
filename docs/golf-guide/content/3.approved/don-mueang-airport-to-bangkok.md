# Don Mueang Airport to Bangkok — What Golf Travellers Need to Know

Don Mueang International Airport (DMK) is Bangkok's secondary airport and the primary hub for low-cost carriers operating across Southeast Asia. If you've flown into Bangkok on AirAsia, Nok Air, Lion Air, or a similar budget airline, you've landed here. For golf travellers, the transfer into the city — and onwards to your course — works a little differently than arriving at Suvarnabhumi, and it's worth knowing the specifics before you land with a bag full of clubs.

## How Don Mueang Differs from Suvarnabhumi

The single most important thing to understand is that Don Mueang has **no direct rail link to the city centre**. Suvarnabhumi has the Airport Rail Link, which makes transfers fast and straightforward (though not useful with golf equipment). Don Mueang does not have an equivalent, and as of early 2026, a planned rail connection remains under development and is not operational. Always check the current status of transport options before you travel, as this situation has the potential to change.

Don Mueang sits roughly 25 km north of Bangkok city centre. While that sounds comparable to Suvarnabhumi, the location actually works in your favour for golfers targeting the northern and central courses around the Rangsit corridor, Pathum Thani, and courses along the northern expressway routes.

---

## Transfer Option 1: Metered Taxi

The metered taxi is the most practical option for golfers arriving with equipment.

- Taxis queue at the official rank on the arrivals level; look for the staffed desk that prints a queue ticket
- Fares to central Bangkok (Sukhumvit, Silom) typically run **200–400 THB** depending on traffic and your exact destination
- Expressway tolls are charged on top of the meter and usually add **50–100 THB**; the driver will ask whether you want to use the expressway — the answer is almost always yes
- Insist on the meter at the desk — the driver is required to use it; decline offers from touts in the arrivals hall
- A standard sedan can fit one golf bag in the boot alongside regular luggage; for two bags you may need to ask for a larger vehicle (minivan/van taxis are available from the same rank)

**Journey time:** 30–60 minutes to the city centre in normal traffic; budget 60–90 minutes during morning or evening peak hours.

---

## Transfer Option 2: Grab

Grab (the dominant ride-hailing app across Southeast Asia) operates freely at Don Mueang and is a reliable alternative to metered taxis.

- Download and set up the app before you arrive if you haven't already
- Fares are shown upfront before you confirm, making budgeting straightforward
- Prices are broadly similar to metered taxis including tolls, sometimes slightly higher during surge pricing
- GrabCar standard sedans fit one golf bag comfortably; book GrabCar XL or GrabVan if you're travelling with two bags or a group with luggage

**Practical note:** Grab pickups at Don Mueang depart from a designated zone. Follow in-app instructions for the exact pickup point, as it can differ from the metered taxi rank.

---

## Transfer Option 3: Public Bus (A1 and A2 Routes)

Don Mueang is served by two airport express bus routes:

- **A1** runs to Mo Chit 2 Bus Terminal (BTS Mo Chit interchange)
- **A2** runs to Victory Monument (BTS Victory Monument station)

Fares are low (around 30 THB) and the buses are air-conditioned, making them a practical option for solo travellers with only carry-on luggage.

**For golfers: these buses are not practical with full golf equipment.** There is no storage for oversized bags, and boarding and alighting during stops with a golf travel bag is not feasible. Mention them here for completeness — if you send your clubs ahead via a hotel transfer service or are playing a course that provides rental sets, the bus becomes an option for your own journey.

## Journey Times and Course Proximity

Don Mueang's northern position is a genuine advantage for some golf itineraries:

- Courses in the **Rangsit / Pathum Thani corridor** (e.g. Thai Country Club, Bangkok Golf Club) can be reached in 20–40 minutes with light traffic — significantly quicker than from Suvarnabhumi
- City-centre hotels in **Sukhumvit or the CBD** are 45–75 minutes in normal traffic
- Courses on Bangkok's **eastern side** (Bang Na, Bangplee) are further and involve crossing the city — factor in 90+ minutes in peak traffic

If your golf itinerary is weighted towards northern courses, arriving at Don Mueang can actually save time compared to routing through Suvarnabhumi.

## Practical Tips for Golfers

1. **Pre-arrange your hotel's shuttle if available.** Some hotels north of the city offer a Don Mueang pickup service; call ahead.
2. **Book a van or XL ride if travelling with two bags.** Don't assume a standard sedan will fit both bags — confirm before loading.
3. **Avoid peak hours if you can.** The expressway from Don Mueang feeds into heavy Bangkok traffic; an early-morning arrival can save 30 minutes or more.
4. **Confirm expressway use upfront.** If the driver takes the tollway, you pay the tolls; this is standard and correct — just be aware so you have small change ready.
5. **Allow extra time on return trips.** Traffic heading north out of Bangkok during the afternoon peak (3–7 pm) can be severe; build in a buffer before your departure.
6. **Check if your golf course offers transfers.** Several Bangkok-area courses offer or can arrange airport pickup; it's worth asking when you book your tee time.
