# Is It Cheaper to Book Golf in Thailand Last Minute?

**Sometimes — but not reliably.** The idea that courses discount unsold tee times at the last minute is partly true, but it depends on the day, the season, and the course. In many cases, waiting to book simply means paying the same rate with fewer good times left to choose from. Understanding when last-minute actually works will save you from a frustrating morning scrambling for a tee time.

---

## Twilight Rates vs True Last-Minute Discounts

These are two different things, and confusing them leads to disappointment.

**Twilight rates** are structured discounts offered for tee times after a fixed cut-off — typically 2:00 pm or 3:00 pm depending on the course. These lower rates are published in advance and available to anyone who books them. They are not last-minute deals; they are time-of-day pricing. If you want a guaranteed lower green fee, booking a twilight slot in advance is the most reliable way to get one.

**True last-minute discounts** — where a course drops its rate on the day to fill an unsold spot — are uncommon in Thailand. A small number of courses use platforms such as Deemples or GolfNow to release discounted same-day tee times, but this is far from standard practice. Most clubs hold their published rates regardless of how full the sheet is.

---

## When Last-Minute Booking Works

There are situations where walking up or booking same-day poses little risk:

1. **Weekdays during low season (March–November)** — Courses outside Bangkok's most popular circuit rarely fill their midweek mornings, and you may find slots available with no prior booking at all.
2. **Less popular or newer courses** — Courses still building their reputation often have availability on short notice throughout the week.
3. **Large supply, flexible plans** — Bangkok has more than 50 courses within an hour of the city centre. If you are genuinely flexible about where you play, you can almost always find *somewhere* to tee off same-day, even if your first choice is full.

---

## When Last-Minute Doesn't Work

Do not rely on availability in these situations:

1. **Weekends year-round** — Bangkok's most popular courses fill their weekend morning slots well in advance. Same-day availability on a Saturday or Sunday is rare for prime tee times.
2. **Peak season (December–February)** — The cool-season months draw a combination of local golfers and visiting tourists. Courses at better facilities can be fully booked days out, and same-day rates are not discounted because demand is high.
3. **Public holidays and long weekends** — These are the hardest days to find any availability last minute, at any price.

---

## The Smart Strategy

Rather than gambling on last-minute availability, use this approach:

1. **Book your preferred course in advance** for the day and time you actually want.
2. **Identify a backup course** — something equally accessible — so you have an option if your first choice is unavailable.
3. **Book a twilight slot** if budget is the priority. You get a confirmed tee time at a lower rate, without relying on a discount that may never materialise.

The combination of advance booking and a backup option gives you flexibility without the uncertainty of waiting until the day.
