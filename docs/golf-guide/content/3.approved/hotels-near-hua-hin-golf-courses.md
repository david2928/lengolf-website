# Best Hotels Near Hua Hin Golf Courses

Hua Hin sits roughly three hours south of Bangkok on Thailand's Gulf coast, and it has quietly become one of the country's most complete golf destinations. Unlike Bangkok, where courses are scattered across a sprawling city, Hua Hin's top layouts are clustered in the hills and valleys just west of town. That compact geography means where you stay has a genuine impact on how smoothly your golf trip runs — the difference between a five-minute drive to the first tee and an hour-long transfer is real.

The town itself adds to the appeal. Hua Hin has been a royal retreat since the 1920s, which gives it a more relaxed, polished feel than many Thai beach towns. Beachfront hotels, good restaurants, night markets, and a functioning town centre all sit within a small area. For golfers, it offers the rare combination of world-class courses and a genuinely pleasant place to spend the non-golf hours.

---

## Where to Stay: Three Zones to Know

### 1. West of Town — Closest to the Courses

The hills and valleys west of Hua Hin town are where most of the serious golf happens. [Black Mountain Golf Club](/guide/black-mountain-golf-club-hua-hin) sits roughly five miles outside town — about a 25-minute drive from the beachfront — and [Banyan Golf Club](/guide/banyan-golf-club-hua-hin) is in the same general direction. Accommodation in this western zone puts you as close as ten minutes from the clubhouse, which matters when you have a 7 am tee time.

This area tends to offer mid-range hotels, serviced residences, and villa-style properties rather than large beach resorts. Trade-offs include being further from the sea and town restaurants, but for golfers playing two rounds a day the proximity advantage is hard to argue against.

### 2. Hua Hin Town Centre

The town centre sits between the beach and the western hills, making it a reasonable compromise for golfers who also want easy access to restaurants, the night market, and the old railway station area. Most courses are 20–30 minutes away — not ideal for an early tee time, but manageable if you plan ahead.

Hotels in this zone range from guesthouses to comfortable mid-range properties. If you are travelling with a partner or family who will not be playing every round, the town centre location keeps everyone happy.

### 3. Beachfront

Hua Hin's beachfront hotels are the most prestigious addresses in town, and some are genuinely excellent properties. The downside for golfers is distance: the beach runs north to south along the east side of town, while the courses are to the west. A beachfront hotel typically adds 15–20 minutes each way to your course transfer, which compounds quickly over a multi-round trip.

If your priority is a luxury resort experience and golf is one of several activities, the beachfront works. If golf is the primary reason you are there, staying closer to the courses is a better use of your time and energy.

## What to Look for in a Hua Hin Golf Hotel

Not every hotel in Hua Hin is set up for golfers. Before booking, check for these specifics:

1. **Golf transfers** — Does the hotel offer a shuttle to the major courses, or can they reliably arrange one? This matters more than proximity if you do not plan to rent a car.
2. **Club storage** — Secure, dry storage for bags between rounds is a basic expectation that not every property meets.
3. **Early breakfast** — A 7 am tee time means leaving by 6:30 at the latest. Confirm the kitchen opens early enough, or that grab-and-go options are available.
4. **Late check-out flexibility** — If you are playing an afternoon round on your departure day, late check-out or luggage storage saves a lot of logistical stress.
5. **Laundry service** — If you are playing three or four days straight, same-day laundry turnaround is worth paying for.

## Overnight vs Day Trip from Bangkok

Bangkok to Hua Hin is around three hours each way by road or rail, which makes a day trip technically possible but practically exhausting. Add a round of golf in the middle and you are looking at a 14-hour day with two long transfers and very little time to enjoy the destination.

The honest recommendation: if you are going to Hua Hin to play golf, stay at least two nights. One night gives you a single full day on the course without the punishing same-day return. Two or three nights lets you play multiple courses at a relaxed pace, explore the town in the evening, and actually recover between rounds. The accommodation options in Hua Hin are good enough — and affordable enough — that there is no real reason to treat it as a day trip.

## Best Time to Visit

The best months for golf in Hua Hin are November through February. Temperatures are lower (typically 25–30°C), humidity is manageable, and rainfall is minimal. This window also coincides with peak tourist season, so accommodation fills up and prices rise — book early if you are travelling in December or January.

March and April bring rising heat. May through October is the wetter period on the Gulf coast, though Hua Hin generally receives less rainfall than the Andaman side of Thailand during the main monsoon months. Golf is still possible in the shoulder months, but expect softer course conditions and the occasional disrupted round.

For a full breakdown of when to play across Thailand, see the [best time to play golf in Thailand](/guide/best-time-play-golf-thailand) guide.

## Booking Tips

1. **Book accommodation and tee times together** — Course availability and hotel availability both tighten over the Nov–Feb peak. Confirming both at the same time avoids the frustration of having one without the other.
2. **Check transfer arrangements before you commit** — If a hotel advertises golf transfers, ask specifically which courses and at what times. "Golf transfers available" sometimes means on request at full taxi rates.
3. **Read reviews from golfers specifically** — General traveller reviews may not flag issues like slow breakfast service or inconvenient bag storage that matter a great deal to golfers with early starts.
4. **Consider a golf package** — Several operators offer Hua Hin packages that combine accommodation with pre-booked rounds at Black Mountain, Banyan, and other courses. These can simplify logistics and sometimes offer better value than booking separately.
