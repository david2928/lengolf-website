# Thailand Golf Trip Cost — Full Budget Breakdown

A week of golf in Thailand costs roughly **$600–$2,500 USD on the ground** depending on your choices. That range is wide because the variables — which courses you play, where you sleep, how you get around — differ dramatically. This guide breaks down every line item so you can build a realistic budget before you book.

Flights are excluded throughout; this covers on-the-ground costs from arrival to departure.

## Green Fees in Detail

Green fees are the biggest variable in your budget. Rates at Bangkok-area courses run from about 1,500 THB at value public courses on weekdays up to 5,000 THB or more at premium resort or private-access courses.

Three factors drive the price gap:

1. **Day of the week.** Weekend rates at most courses are 300–600 THB higher than weekday rates. Scheduling your rounds Monday–Friday stretches your budget significantly.
2. **Course tier.** Bangkok's top-ranked courses carry premium fees. See [best golf courses near Bangkok](/guide/best-golf-courses-near-bangkok) for a ranked overview with fee context.
3. **Season.** November through February is peak season — cooler temperatures, drier conditions, and higher demand. Some courses apply a high-season surcharge. See the [complete Thailand golf guide](/golf-in-thailand-guide) for seasonal planning advice.

## Caddie Costs — Mandatory, Not Optional

Caddies are compulsory at virtually every golf course in Thailand. Budget 400–600 THB for the caddie fee itself, plus a tip of 400–500 THB after your round. The tip is not formally required but is considered standard practice and is factored into every realistic budget.

Total caddie cost per round: **800–1,100 THB (~$22–30 USD)**.

Experienced caddies provide real value — course knowledge, club selection advice, and help locating balls — so this is money well spent rather than a grudging surcharge.

## Transport Costs

Getting from central Bangkok to most courses takes 45–90 minutes depending on traffic and course location. Your options:

1. **Grab (rideshare).** Most convenient for solo travellers. Expect 300–550 THB each way from Suvarnabhumi area; more from central Bangkok depending on destination.
2. **Course shuttle.** Some resort courses run scheduled transfers from central Bangkok. Check when booking — this can simplify logistics and occasionally costs less than private hire.
3. **Private car hire for the day.** If playing multiple stops or travelling as a group, a full-day car hire (2,000–3,500 THB) can be more economical per person.

Budget travellers typically spend 600–800 THB per day on transport (return trip); mid-range golfers 800–1,100 THB.

## Hotel Costs

Bangkok offers accommodation across every price point. Golf-relevant options:

1. **Budget/guesthouse:** 1,000–2,000 THB/night (~$27–54). Clean and functional; limited golf-specific services.
2. **Mid-range (3–4 star):** 2,500–5,000 THB/night (~$68–135). Good location options near transport corridors.
3. **Luxury (5 star):** 8,000–20,000+ THB/night (~$216–540+). Full concierge, airport transfers, and dedicated golf booking services.

Resort courses with on-site accommodation can offer value by bundling rooms with preferential tee times. See [best Bangkok hotels for golfers](/guide/best-bangkok-hotels-golfers) for property-specific recommendations.

## Food and Incidentals

Thai street food and local restaurants are exceptional value — a full meal at a local restaurant runs 80–200 THB. Western-style restaurants and hotel dining push costs to 400–800 THB per meal. Budget 500–800 THB/day for food on a tight plan; 1,000–1,500 THB/day covers meals at a comfortable mid-range level.

Incidentals to account for:

- Golf ball top-ups: 100–400 THB for a sleeve at pro shops
- Sun protection, sports drinks at the course: 100–200 THB/day
- Laundry if on an extended trip: 200–400 THB per wash

## How to Reduce Costs

Seven practical ways to keep the budget down:

1. **Play weekdays only.** Weekend premiums are real; shift your schedule where possible.
2. **Book low or shoulder season.** March–May and September–October offer lower fees and uncrowded courses (heat and occasional rain are the trade-off).
3. **Rent clubs locally rather than paying airline baggage fees.** Rental sets at most courses cost 1,000–2,500 THB per round — often less than checked baggage charges for a return flight. Read [bring your clubs or rent in Thailand](/guide/bring-golf-clubs-thailand-or-rent) for a full cost comparison.
4. **Use Grab instead of taxis.** Metered taxis can be inconsistent; Grab prices are transparent and typically competitive.
5. **Eat where locals eat.** The quality gap between street food and hotel dining is small; the price gap is large.
6. **Book tee times in advance online.** Walk-in rates at some courses are higher than pre-booked rates.
7. **Mix simulator rounds into your schedule.** On travel days, weather days, or when you want extra range time without the full per-round cost, indoor simulator sessions offer a cost-effective alternative (see below).
