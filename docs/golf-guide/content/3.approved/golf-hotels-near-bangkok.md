# Golf Hotels Near Bangkok — Stay and Play Packages

Planning a golf trip around Bangkok does not have to mean juggling separate hotel bookings, tee time reservations, and transport logistics. Stay-and-play packages bundle accommodation with rounds of golf — and sometimes transfers and caddie fees — into a single arrangement, making it easier to budget and plan. Whether you want a resort escape south of Bangkok or a city-based base with easy course access, the Bangkok region has options to suit most golfer profiles.

## Two Types of Golf Hotel Arrangements

### 1. Resort-Integrated Golf Hotels

The most classic stay-and-play experience places you directly at — or next to — a golf resort. Here, your hotel and your tee times are managed by the same operator or a closely affiliated partner. You wake up, walk to the clubhouse, play, and return to your room without arranging transport.

The main resort golf destinations within reach of Bangkok are:

- **Hua Hin** (~3 hours south): Thailand's original seaside resort town. Home to world-class courses including [Black Mountain Golf Club](/guide/black-mountain-golf-club-hua-hin) and Banyan Golf Club (sometimes marketed as Pineapple Valley). Several resorts here are built around or adjacent to their own courses.
- **Pattaya** (~1.5 hours east): A denser cluster of courses, some attached to larger resort properties, with a range of accommodation from budget to five-star.

These destinations work best for trips of two nights or more, allowing you to play multiple rounds without wasting time in transit.

### 2. Bangkok City Hotels with Golf Arrangements

Bangkok itself has over 50 golf courses within roughly one hour of the city centre. Many mid-range and upscale city hotels — particularly business hotels in areas like Sukhumvit and Silom — offer golf concierge services rather than integrated resort packages. This typically means the hotel will arrange a tee time and transfer to a nearby course as part of a stay-and-play add-on.

This model suits golfers who want to combine golf with city sightseeing, business meetings, or other travel, using Bangkok as a central hub rather than committing to a resort town.

## What a Stay-and-Play Package Typically Includes

Package contents vary significantly by property and price tier, but a well-structured stay-and-play deal generally covers:

1. Accommodation (usually one room per night, per two golfers)
2. A set number of rounds (commonly one round per night stayed)
3. Green fees at one or more designated courses
4. Shared buggy (cart) rental
5. Breakfast

Packages less commonly include caddie fees, which at many Thai courses are mandatory or strongly expected. Always confirm whether caddies are included or billed separately — this can add 300–500 THB per round to your real cost.

## Hua Hin: The Primary Resort Golf Destination Near Bangkok

Of all the day-trip or weekend destinations from Bangkok, Hua Hin offers the most established resort golf infrastructure. The town has been a retreat for Bangkok residents for decades, and its golf scene reflects that — courses here tend to be well-maintained, resort-adjacent, and accustomed to international visitors.

Green fees in the area run roughly 1,500–5,000+ THB depending on the course and season, with premium courses at the higher end on weekends. For a full breakdown of what to play, see our guide to the [best golf courses near Bangkok](/guide/best-golf-courses-near-bangkok).

The 3-hour drive from Bangkok makes Hua Hin viable as a long weekend destination: depart Friday evening, play Saturday and Sunday, return Monday. Alternatively, some golfers use a private transfer or a combination of bus and hotel shuttle.

## How to Find and Compare Packages

There are three main routes to booking a stay-and-play package near Bangkok:

1. **Direct with the resort**: The most transparent option. Resort websites — particularly for the larger Hua Hin properties — often list package rates with clear inclusions. You can negotiate directly for group bookings.
2. **Golf travel specialists**: Agencies that focus on Thailand golf trips can bundle multi-course itineraries across different resorts, often at better rates than booking each element separately. They handle tee sheet logistics and can advise on course conditions and seasonal availability.
3. **General booking platforms**: OTA platforms sometimes carry golf packages, particularly for large resort properties. Coverage of smaller or boutique golf resorts can be patchy, so compare against direct rates.

## What to Watch For

Before confirming any package, check these points:

1. **Peak season surcharges**: High season (roughly November–February) brings higher demand and higher prices. Weekend rates are almost always higher than weekday rates.
2. **Caddie fees**: As noted, these are frequently excluded even from inclusive-sounding packages. Verify in writing.
3. **Buggy vs. walking**: Some courses restrict walking; others charge for buggies on top of the green fee. Packages do not always specify which applies.
4. **Transfer logistics**: Resort packages may include one return transfer from Bangkok. If you are arriving from the airport or plan multiple excursions, confirm transport costs separately.
5. **Course rotation**: Multi-night packages at a single resort sometimes lock you into the same course. If you want variety, look for packages that include access to partner courses.

## Better Value Than Booking Separately?

It depends on the destination and the time of year. For Hua Hin and Pattaya resort properties, packages frequently offer genuine savings — the resort has strong incentive to fill rooms and tee sheets simultaneously, and bundled pricing reflects that. The saving is often most visible on accommodation rather than green fees, since courses in these areas tend to have less flexible rack-rate pricing.

For city-based golf arrangements in Bangkok, the maths are less consistent. A hotel's concierge-arranged tee time may carry a small premium over booking direct with the course. In these cases, the value is in convenience rather than cost.

During peak season and for large groups, booking independently and negotiating directly with courses often beats any packaged rate you can find online.
