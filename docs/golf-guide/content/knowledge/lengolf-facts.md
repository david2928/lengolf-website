## Bangkok Golf — General Facts

- **Bangkok golf courses**: 40+ golf courses within an hour of central Bangkok
- **Caddie system**: Caddies are mandatory at most Thai golf courses (not optional)
- **Typical caddie fee**: 400–600 THB per round (paid to the course at check-in, not directly to the caddie) — verified 2025/2026
- **Typical caddie tip**: 400–500 THB standard; 500–600 THB mid-range/well-regarded course; 600–1,000 THB premium/private club; 700–1,000 THB exceptional service — verified 2025/2026
- **Green fees range (Bangkok)**: 1,500–5,000+ THB depending on course and day/time
- **Green fees range (weekday vs weekend)**: Weekend rates typically 20–40% higher than weekday rates
- **Dress code**: Collared shirts required at virtually all Thai golf courses; shorts acceptable but must be tailored (no cargo shorts or denim)
- **Golf shoes**: Soft spikes or spikeless required; many courses rent shoes

## Golf Travel — Airlines & Baggage

- **Golf bag as checked baggage**: Permitted on all major airlines to Bangkok; most count as oversized baggage
- **Typical oversized baggage fee**: USD 50–200 per flight depending on airline and route
- **Hard case vs soft case**: Hard cases offer better protection; soft travel bags are lighter and cheaper
- **Airlines with good golf bag policies** (as of 2026): Thai Airways, Emirates, Qatar Airways tend to be golf-traveller friendly — always verify current policy directly with airline before booking

## Seasonal / Weather

- **Best time to play golf in Bangkok**: November–February (cool season, low humidity, no rain)
- **Rainy season**: May–October; afternoon thunderstorms common; morning tee times recommended
- **Hot season**: March–May; temperatures 35–40°C; early morning tee times essential
- **Best time of day**: 6–9am tee times recommended year-round to avoid heat; many courses offer twilight rates after 2–3pm

## Getting Around Bangkok for Golf

- **BTS Skytrain**: Does not reach most golf courses; useful for getting to Ekkamai/Mo Chit bus terminals or hotel areas
- **Grab**: Most convenient option for golfers; can request large vehicles; accepts golf bags
- **Golf course shuttle**: Some hotels and courses offer shuttle services, especially in Hua Hin
- **Taxi**: Available but negotiate fare or insist on meter; not all taxis accept golf bags

## Booking

- **GolfNow**: Operates in Thailand; covers some Bangkok courses but not comprehensive
- **Direct booking**: Most courses accept direct phone/email bookings; some have online systems
- **Advance booking**: Weekend tee times at popular courses book 1–2 weeks in advance; weekday times often available same-day
- **Typical booking window**: 7–14 days in advance recommended for popular courses

## Simulator vs Outdoor Golf

- **Cost comparison**: Golf simulator (LENGOLF): ~800–1,500 THB/hour per bay. Outdoor round: 1,500–5,000 THB green fee + caddie fee + transport
- **Simulator advantages**: No weather risk, no transport time, play at night, beginner-friendly, no caddie required
- **Outdoor advantages**: Real course experience, social/networking, fresh air, authentic Thai golf culture
