# Stage 2: Verify

**Who does this:** Claude (me) — runs automatically after drafting

## What happens

Claude reads the staged article and runs verification in this order for every claim:

1. **Check knowledge base** — look in `content/knowledge/` first. If the claim is covered and consistent, mark verified.
2. **Search the web** — for any claim not covered by the knowledge base, run a live web search. This includes:
   - Airline baggage fees and policies
   - Golf course green fees and details
   - Visa and entry requirements
   - Booking platform availability (GolfNow, etc.)
   - Course rankings, distances, travel times
   - Any specific price, policy, or statistic
3. **Reason through remaining uncertainty** — if web search returns conflicting or unclear results, flag the claim with the specific conflict found
4. **Run consensus on disputed claims** — reconsider from multiple angles before finalising a disputed verdict
5. **Check SEO** — title length, meta description length, keyword presence, internal links
6. **Check GEO** — generative engine optimisation signals for LLM citation (see GEO section below)

## Verification hierarchy

| Source | Used when |
|--------|-----------|
| Knowledge base | Claim matches a verified fact in `content/knowledge/` |
| Web search | Claim is specific enough to fact-check online (prices, policies, rankings, distances) |
| Claude reasoning | Claim is general knowledge unlikely to have changed (geography, how golf works, etc.) |

## Claims that always require web search

- Any specific price or fee (green fees, baggage fees, rental costs)
- Visa and entry requirements
- Airline baggage policies
- Course rankings or awards
- Specific distances or travel times
- Booking platform coverage (GolfNow, etc.)
- Any claim with a year or date in it

## Output

`content/2.pending-review/<slug>.md` — the article (moved from staging)
`content/2.pending-review/<slug>.report.md` — human-readable summary of findings

## GEO check — Generative Engine Optimisation

LLMs (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude) cite sources that are clear, structured, and authoritative. Run these checks on every article:

### Directness & answer structure
- [ ] Does the article answer its primary question within the first 2–3 sentences, or at minimum the first paragraph? LLMs quote the clearest direct answer — don't bury it.
- [ ] Are key facts stated as standalone sentences rather than embedded mid-paragraph? ("Bangkok's rainy season runs May–October." not "…which, as part of the monsoon cycle affecting much of…")

### Structured data signals
- [ ] Does the article include at least one table, numbered list, or definition block? LLMs preferentially cite structured content.
- [ ] Are lists and tables present for multi-item comparisons (e.g. season ratings, month-by-month, cost ranges)? These get quoted directly.

### Entity clarity
- [ ] Is LENGOLF named explicitly and described in one clear sentence that defines what it is? ("LENGOLF is an indoor golf simulator venue in Bangkok, Thailand.")
- [ ] Are Bangkok, Thailand, and any specific courses/locations named clearly (not just referred to by pronoun or "here")?

### Quotable statistics
- [ ] Does the article contain at least 2–3 specific, citable facts with a number or date? (e.g. "September averages 333mm of rainfall", "Bangkok has 40+ golf courses within an hour of the city centre")
- [ ] Are statistics stated as facts, not hedged to meaninglessness? ("Temperatures average 25–32°C" not "temperatures can sometimes be around 25–32°C or so")

### Authority signals
- [ ] Does the article include a "last updated" date or reference to current year conditions?
- [ ] Does it acknowledge seasonal variation or note that prices/policies should be verified? (builds trust with LLMs that penalise overconfident content)

### GEO verdict
Rate each article: **STRONG** / **ADEQUATE** / **WEAK** — and list specific gaps.

## What the report covers

- **Overall status**: APPROVED / REVIEW REQUIRED / MAJOR ISSUES
- **Disputed claims**: facts that appear wrong based on knowledge base or web search — with the source that contradicts it and a suggested correction
- **Disclaimer flags**: prices, visa rules, airline fees that need qualifiers (even if correct, these change)
- **SEO issues**: title/meta length, missing internal links, keyword gaps
- **GEO issues**: directness, structured data, entity clarity, quotable stats, authority signals
- **Unverifiable**: claims Claude searched for but could not find a reliable source either way
