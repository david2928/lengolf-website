## Data Quality Notes

### 1. Three HELD courses — action required

Three of the 14 pipeline-assigned courses cannot be published:

1. **Iyara Park Golf Course** (`iyara-park-golf-course`) — Confirmed permanently closed since ~2012–2013 due to flooding. GolfDD.com lists it explicitly as closed. This slug should be retired from the Bangkok pipeline.

2. **Wangjuntr Golf Park** (`wangjuntr-golf-park`) — Located in Rayong province (Pattaya area), confirmed by 6+ sources. This is a major 54-hole resort complex, not an Ayutthaya course. Re-assign to Pattaya Phase 2 pipeline.

3. **Prapasri Golf Course** (`prapasri-golf-course`) — Located in Sakon Nakhon province, northeastern Thailand (~650 km from Bangkok). It is a small military 9-hole facility. If a Bangkok-area "Prapasri" course was intended (possibly the "Royal Thai Army Prapasri Golf Club" noted on 4moles.com), the pipeline coordinator should clarify the exact course name and location.

### 2. Two significant location corrections in this batch

- **Killien Golf Club**: Pipeline brief said "near Chachoengsao" — confirmed Nakhon Pathom province (Bang Len District), ~65 km west of Bangkok, not east.

- **Northern Rangsit Golf Club**: Pipeline brief said "Rangsit area, Pathum Thani" — the course sits at Phahonyothin Km.58, which is in Wang Noi, Ayutthaya province, just across the provincial border.

### 3. Muang Ake Group — three related courses clarified

All three Muang Ake Group courses are confirmed as distinct venues:
- **Muang Ake Golf Course** (1986, Pathum Thani) — original course, 18 holes, par 72, 6,380 yards
- **Muang Ake Vista Golf Course** (1992, Pathum Thani) — rebranded RSU Vista 2022, 18 holes, par 70, 5,572 yards; has full night-golf floodlighting
- **Muang Ake Wang Noi Golf Course** (2006, Ayutthaya) — rebranded Wang Noi Prestige / The Wangnoi Ayudhaya, 18 holes, par 72, 6,830 yards

The three courses share the Muang Ake brand origin but have different management post-2018.

### 4. Kumlung-Ake — confirmed NOT related to Muang Ake

Kumlung-Ake Golf Course in Loei province is not connected to the Bangkok-area Muang Ake group. The similar name appears coincidental. The Loei course is a 9-hole military/public facility approximately 500 km from Bangkok and not a realistic Bangkok day-trip destination.
